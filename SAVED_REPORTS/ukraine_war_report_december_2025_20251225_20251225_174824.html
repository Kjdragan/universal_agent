Russia-Ukraine War: Situation Report

Developments as of December 25, 2025 (Day 1,400)

Report compiled: December 25, 2025

1.Executive Summary

As December 2025 draws to a close, the Russia-Ukraine war enters its fourth year since the full-scale invasion, with Day 1,400 marked on December 24-25, 2025. The conflict shows complex dynamics: gradual Russian territorial advances in eastern Ukraine continue alongside intensive diplomatic efforts mediated by the United States, while both sides exchange long-range strikes targeting critical infrastructure.

Key Development: US and Ukrainian negotiators have agreed on a 20-point peace framework after marathon talks in Florida, representing the most significant diplomatic progress since the war began. However, territorial disputes remain unresolved, and Russia has yet to formally respond to the proposal.

Russia claims to have seized more than 5,100 square kilometers of Ukrainian territory in 2025, though independent analysts dispute some of these claims. Ukrainian forces continue to hold approximately 5,000 square kilometers of Donbas that Russia demands be ceded. The European Union has approved a 90 billion euro loan package for Ukraine, while peace talks intensify through indirect channels involving American, European, Ukrainian, and Russian representatives.

Current Territorial Control: Russia controls approximately 19-20% of Ukrainian territory, including Crimea and parts of Donetsk, Luhansk, Zaporizhzhia, and Kherson oblasts. This represents roughly 117,143 square kilometers (45,229 square miles).

3.Territorial Changes and Military Situation

Russian Territorial Claims and Advances

Russian Claims for 2025:

  • Russian Defence Minister Andrei Belousov: 6,000 sq km (2,300 sq miles) seized
  • Russian commander-in-chief Valery Gerasimov: 6,300 sq km (2,432 sq miles) seized
  • President Vladimir Putin: Claims seizure of more than 5,100 sq km in eastern Donetsk and Vovchansk in northern Kharkiv region

However, the Institute for the Study of War (ISW) provides a more conservative estimate, assessing that Russia has seized no more than 4,984 sq km (1,900 sq miles) containing 196 settlements, rather than the 300 settlements claimed by Russian officials. ISW also observed that satellite and open-source visual evidence contradict some of Putin's specific claims about towns.

Recent Russian Advances (December 16-22, 2025):

  • Russia gained 51 square miles of Ukrainian territory in the past six days
  • Since November 25-December 22, 2025: Russian forces gained 138 square miles
  • Since January 1, 2025: Average monthly rate of Russian gains has been 176 square miles
  • Ukraine's foothold across Kursk and Belgorod regions remained at 3 square miles, unchanged from previous week

Disputed Control Claims: Putin claimed Russian forces hold at least half of Lyman and Kostiantynivka in Donetsk, and Hulyaipole in Zaporizhia. ISW estimates Russian presence at only 7.3% of Hulyaipole, 2.9% of Lyman, and no more than 5% of Kostiantynivka. Even Russian milbloggers claim maximum advances of 7% in Lyman and 11% in Kostiantynivka.

Current Overall Territorial Control

  • Total Russian-controlled territory: Approximately 117,143 square kilometers (45,229 square miles) as of October 28, 2025
  • Percentage of Ukraine: About 19-20% of Ukrainian territory
  • Includes: Crimea (annexed 2014) and parts of Donetsk, Luhansk, Zaporizhzhia, and Kherson oblasts
  • Ukrainian-held Donbas: Approximately 5,000 square kilometers still controlled by Ukraine in Donetsk region

Military Operations and Casualties

Recent Attack Statistics (Week of December 18-24):

  • Russia launched 1,227 drones and 41 missiles at Ukraine
  • Ukraine intercepted 80% of drones and 83% of missiles
  • Overall for past week: Russia launched about 1,300 drones, nearly 1,200 guided aerial bombs, and 9 missiles toward Ukraine

Energy Infrastructure Attacks:

  • Russian forces continue to strike energy infrastructure, particularly targeting critical infrastructure in Chernihiv Oblast
  • Ukraine has lost approximately 64% of its electricity generating capacity (destroyed or occupied) as of 2024
  • Ukraine's energy infrastructure operating at only about 50% of pre-invasion generation capacity as of Fall 2025
  • Russia reported destroying 30% of Ukraine's gas production ahead of winter 2025-2026
  • ISW estimates Russia's strike campaign is cutting Ukraine's power grid east-west, with eastern regions "at the brink" of blackout

Ukrainian Strikes on Russia: Ukraine has targeted Russian refineries, power plants, and energy infrastructure throughout 2025. According to March 2025 estimates by RFE/RL, Ukrainian strikes on Russia's energy sector caused at least 60 billion rubles in damage. As of early October 2025, Ukrainian drone strikes had forced nearly 40% of Russia's oil refining capacity offline, with at least 70% of shutdowns directly linked to these strikes.

4.Peace Talks and Diplomatic Developments

The 20-Point US-Ukraine Peace Framework

The most significant diplomatic development has been the agreement between US and Ukrainian negotiators on a 20-point peace framework after marathon talks in Florida. The plan, revealed by President Zelenskyy on December 24-25, represents a modified version of an earlier 28-point draft that had been seen as supporting many of Russia's main demands.

Key Elements of the Framework:

Territorial Arrangements:

  • Ukraine proposed creating a demilitarized "free economic zone" in portions of Donetsk still controlled by Ukraine
  • Ukrainian military would withdraw from this territory, which would become a demilitarized zone monitored by international forces
  • Any arrangement must be contingent upon a referendum allowing Ukrainian people to determine their own fate
  • Ukrainian police would remain in the zone; only Ukrainians living in Ukrainian territory could vote (excluding those under occupation)
  • 60 days required for referendum process, during which hostilities should stop
  • Contact line should be frozen once agreement is signed

Zaporizhzhia Nuclear Power Plant:

  • US proposal: Consortium with Ukraine, Russia, and US each having equal stake
  • Ukraine rejects joint management with Russia, calling it "very inappropriate and not entirely realistic"
  • Ukrainian counterproposal: Russian withdrawal from plant, with Ukraine and US splitting it 50/50
  • Occupied city of Enerhodar to be demilitarized free economic zone
  • Billions in investment needed to restart plant and restore adjacent dam

Security Guarantees:

  • Ukraine to receive "strong" security guarantees mirroring NATO's Article 5
  • Separate bilateral document with US to outline guarantees and conditions for response to renewed Russian aggression
  • Mechanism to utilize satellite technology and early warning systems for ceasefire monitoring
  • Ukraine allowed to maintain military at 800,000 during peacetime
  • No requirement to recognize occupied territories as de facto Russian
  • No requirement to give up NATO bid (Ukraine can keep constitution enshrining NATO membership goal)

Economic Provisions:

  • Accelerating free trade agreement between Ukraine and US once agreement signed
  • US wants same deal with Russia
  • Ukraine seeks short-term privileged access to European market
  • Robust global development package targeting $800 billion through equity, grants, loans, and private-sector contributions
  • Funds for reconstruction of destroyed territories
  • Specific date for EU inclusion to be nailed down

Humanitarian Issues:

  • All prisoners since 2014 to be released at once
  • Civilian detainees, political prisoners, and children to be returned to Ukraine
  • Elections to be held after signing of agreement

Russian Position and Response

Russia has given no indication it will agree to any kind of withdrawal from land seized in Ukraine. Moscow insists Ukraine relinquish the remaining territory it still holds in Donbas. Russia has captured most of Luhansk and about 70% of Donetsk.

Russian Demands:

  • Ukraine should cede 5,000 square kilometers of Donbas still controlled by Ukraine
  • Kyiv should officially renounce intention to join NATO
  • Russia to keep all regions of Donetsk, Luhansk, Zaporizhia, and Kherson in full, in addition to Crimea

Kremlin spokesperson Dmitry Peskov stated Moscow would "formulate" its position based on information from Russian presidential envoy Kirill Dmitriev, who met with US envoys in Florida. Putin's top foreign policy aide Yury Ushakov said changes made by European countries and Ukraine to US proposals "do not improve the document and do not improve the possibility of achieving long-term peace."

International Support for Ukraine

European Union Financial Support:

  • EU Council approved 90 billion euro ($106 billion) loan to Ukraine over two years
  • Ukraine to receive at least 45 billion euros ($53 billion) annually over next two years
  • Funds to be repaid only from Russian funds, though EU refused to directly connect 210 billion euros in frozen Russian state assets to the loan
  • Decision came after Italy, Bulgaria, Malta, and Belgium vetoed plan to use frozen Russian assets as collateral

United States Position:

  • US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner led negotiations in Miami with Ukrainian delegation and Russian envoy Kirill Dmitriev
  • US Secretary of State Marco Rubio: "Progress made but have a ways to go"
  • "Only a negotiated settlement can end this war"
  • Trump administration no longer sending military assistance but agreed to sell weapons to Ukraine, paid for by EU, Canada, Australia, and Japan

Additional Diplomatic Developments

  • French President Emmanuel Macron said he spoke with NATO chief Mark Rutte about Ukraine and "coalition of the willing"; work to continue in January 2025 to provide Ukraine with "solid security guarantees"
  • India's Ministry of External Affairs said 202 Indian nationals were recruited into Russian armed forces during war; 26 killed and 7 missing according to Russian authorities
  • Sweden's customs service boarded Russian freighter Adler in Swedish waters for inspection; vessel owners on EU sanctions list

5.Humanitarian Impact

Civilian Casualties and Displacement

Recent Civilian Deaths (December 18-25):

  • December 19: Woman killed in car near Odesa; three children injured in same incident
  • December 20: Seven people killed and 15 wounded in Odesa port attacks
  • December 22: 49-year-old man and 42-year-old woman killed in Kharkiv region (Izyum); one killed in Donetsk, one in Zaporizhia
  • December 24-25: Three people including two police officers killed in Moscow explosion

Energy Crisis: Russian attacks continue to devastate Ukraine's energy infrastructure. The city of Odesa has suffered chronic power outages since December 13. In Chernihiv and other regions, energy infrastructure has suffered "extensive damage." Overall, approximately 180,000 consumers were left without electricity across five regions (Mykolaiv, Zaporizhia, Cherkasy, Dnipropetrovsk, and Sumy) as of December 19.

Environmental Impact:

  • Sunflower oil spill contaminated shoreline around Odesa after damage to storage tanks from Russian attacks on port infrastructure
  • Pivdenny port temporarily closed to assist with cleanup
  • Conservationists warned of wildlife killed by contamination

Detentions and Deportations

  • Ukraine accused Russian forces of illegally detaining about 50 residents of Hrabovske village in Sumy region
  • These residents were forcibly deported to Russian territory
    • <>This continues a pattern documented by international organizations of forced deportations since 2022

Prisoners of War and Foreign Fighters

  • Colombian man Oscar Mauricio Blanco Lopez, 42, sentenced to 19 years in prison by Russian-backed court in occupied Donetsk for fighting for Ukrainian army
  • British man Hayden Davies sentenced to 13 years in maximum security prison camp for being paid mercenary fighting for Ukraine
  • Belarusian man Sergei Yeremeyev jailed for 22 years for allegedly blowing up two trains in Siberia for Ukraine
  • Peace framework calls for release of all prisoners since 2014, return of civilian detainees, political prisoners, and children

Long-term Humanitarian Statistics

Displacement: 10.6 million displaced Ukrainians, representing 24% of Ukraine's pre-invasion population of 44 million.

Population Under Occupation: As of 2024, approximately 3 to 3.5 million Ukrainians are estimated to be living under Russian occupation. Since the invasion, the occupied territories have lost roughly half of their population.

Occupation Conditions: According to the United Nations, Russia is committing severe violations in occupied Ukraine, including arbitrary detention, enforced disappearance, torture, crackdown on peaceful protests, and suppression of Ukrainian language and culture. Ukrainian children have been subjected to indoctrination with Russian nationalism, and schools are forced to teach Russian curriculum justifying the invasion. From July 2024, anyone in occupied Ukraine without a Russian passport can be imprisoned as a "foreign citizen."

Casualty Estimates

Russian Casualties: Multiple estimates provide varying figures for Russian killed and injured:

  • April 2025 (SACEUR Cavoli): Specific figures not provided in available sources
  • June 2025 (CSIS): 950,000 killed or injured, including 250,000 killed and 700,000 injured
  • June 2025 (UK Defense Ministry): Specific figures not fully provided
  • Meduza and Mediazona: 219,000 Russian soldiers killed
  • September 2025 (British spy chief Richard Moore): 600,000+ casualties, including 240,000 killed
  • October 2025 (UK Ministry of Defense): Specific figures not fully provided

Ukrainian Casualties:

  • December 2024 estimate by President Trump: Specific figures not provided in available sources
  • CSIS June 2025 estimate: Somewhere between 60,000 and 100,000 killed and 300,000-340,000 injured
  • 15,954 Ukrainian soldiers killed (including 13,145 killed in territories controlled by Ukrainian government and 2,809 killed in Ukrainian territories controlled by Russia)

Equipment Losses: According to May 2025 estimates, Russia has lost at least 10,000 ground combat vehicles (including more than 3,000 tanks), nearly 250 aircraft and helicopters, and more than 10 naval vessels since the war's start.
