### 2. UTHealth Houston Genetic Counseling Program (UTGCP)

**Program:** University of Texas Genetic Counseling Program
**Location:** MD Anderson Cancer Center/UTHealth Houston
**Website:** https://gsbs.uth.edu/genetic-counseling

**What they offer:**
- Observer Program through McGovern Medical School
- Clinical shadowing at MD Anderson Clinical Cancer Genetics Program
- Exposure to cancer genetic counseling

**Requirements:**
- High school diploma or higher (you meet this with your bachelor's)
- Resume/CV
- Proof of immunizations
- Background check clearance
- Brief statement of interest

**How to apply:**
1. Visit: https://med.uth.edu/gme/trainee-resources/visiting-trainees/observers
2. Complete the observer application
3. Submit required documents (resume, immunizations, background check)
4. Once approved, you'll be assigned to a clinical service

**Important note:**
- This program requires direct exposure for admission
- Counts toward the three-letter-of-recommendation requirement
- Provides networking with faculty mentors

## üìã Step-by-Step Guide: How to Arrange Shadowing in Houston

### Step 1: Prepare Your Materials
- Update your resume/CV (highlight science/health experience)
- Draft a professional introduction email
- Prepare a brief statement of interest

### Step 2: Initial Contact
Send an email with:
```
Subject: Shadowing/Informational Interview Request - [Your Name]

Dear [Contact Name],

My name is [Name] and I am a recent graduate with a B.S. in Biomedical Sciences 
and a minor in Genetics from [University]. I am exploring a career in genetic 
counseling and would appreciate the opportunity to shadow genetic counselors or 
schedule an informational interview to learn more about the profession.

I am particularly interested in [specific area: prenatal/cancer/pediatric genetics] 
and would value learning about the daily work of genetic counselors at [Institution].

I have attached my resume for your review. Thank you for considering my request.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

### Step 3: Follow Up
- Wait 1-2 weeks for response
- Send a polite follow-up email if no response
- Be persistent but professional

### Step 4: Prepare for Shadowing
- Research HIPAA and patient privacy
- Prepare professional questions to ask
- Dress professionally
- Arrive early
- Take notes (when appropriate)

### Step 5: After Shadowing
- Send a thank-you email within 24 hours
- Mention specific things you learned
- Ask if they know other counselors you could contact
- Stay in touch for future opportunities

## üìù Sample Questions to Ask During Shadowing/Informational Interviews

### About the Career:
- What does a typical day look like for you?
- What drew you to genetic counseling?
- What are the most rewarding aspects of your job?
- What are the biggest challenges?

### About Education:
- What advice do you have for applying to master's programs?
- What experiences strengthened your application?
- How did you prepare for the ABGC exam?
- What should I look for in a graduate program?

### About Houston/Your Institution:
- What makes the genetic counseling community in Houston unique?
- What opportunities are there for specialization here?
- How would you describe the work environment at this institution?

### About the Application Process:
- What do admissions committees look for?
- How important is shadowing experience?
- What sets successful applicants apart?
- Can you recommend other counselors I should speak with?

### Institutional Policies:
- **Houston Methodist:** Does NOT offer shadowing through their volunteer program
- Many hospitals have strict policies about observers
- HIPAA/privacy requirements may limit patient interaction
- Some institutions may require vaccination records/background checks

### Timing:
- **Summer programs:** Apply in spring (February-April)
- **Year-round opportunities:** Contact 2-3 months in advance
- **Competition:** Limited spots, especially at prestigious institutions

### Requirements You May Need:
- Professional liability insurance (rarely required for observation)
- Immunization records
- Background check
- HIPAA training
- Signed confidentiality agreements
