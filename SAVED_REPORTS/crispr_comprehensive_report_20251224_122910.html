Phase 3 Trials & Late-Stage Developments

Phase 3 NTLA-2001 (Intellia Therapeutics) - ATTR Amyloidosis

Trial Name: MAGNITUDE

Indications: Transthyretin (ATTR) amyloidosis with cardiomyopathy (ATTR-CM) and polyneuropathy (ATTR-PN)

Status: Phase 3 trial actively recruiting and ongoing

Technology: In vivo CRISPR-Cas9 editing delivered via lipid nanoparticles (LNPs) targeting the TTR gene

Significance: Represents one of the most advanced in vivo CRISPR programs, potentially establishing a new paradigm for treating protein-misfolding disorders with a single dose

Phase 1/2 VERVE-101 (Verve Therapeutics) - Familial Hypercholesterolemia

Target: PCSK9 gene in the liver

Technology: Base editing (Adenine Base Editor - ABE)

Results: Phase 1 data showed 47-84% reduction in PCSK9 protein levels at higher doses

Significance: First-in-human base editing trial demonstrating durable lipid lowering with potential for permanent cardiovascular risk reduction after a single treatment

Phase 1/2 PM359 (Prime Medicine) - Chronic Granulomatous Disease

Milestone: First prime editing therapy to enter clinical trials

Indication: Chronic granulomatous disease (CGD), a rare genetic immune disorder

Technology: Prime editing system for precise genome modifications without double-strand breaks

Significance: Represents the clinical translation of David Liu's prime editing platform, potentially treating thousands of diseases with point mutations

Phase 1 BEAM-302 (Beam Therapeutics) - Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency

Indication: Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency (AATD), a genetic disorder causing lung and liver disease

Technology: Base editing delivered via LNPs

Status: Phase 1 trial ongoing

CAR-T Cell Therapy Trials

Multiple CRISPR-edited CAR-T programs are advancing through clinical development:

  • CTX110 (CRISPR Therapeutics): CD19-directed CAR-T for B-cell malignancies
  • CTX130 (CRISPR Therapeutics): CD70-directed CAR-T for renal cell carcinoma and T-cell malignancies
  • ALLO-501/501A (Allogeneic CAR-T programs using CRISPR for host-versus-graft prevention)

Clinical Hold Update

NTLA-2002 (Intellia) - Hereditary Angioedema (HAE):

The FDA placed a clinical hold on the Phase 1/2 trial of NTLA-2002 following reports of liver complications in a patient receiving a different therapy (NTLA-2001 for ATTR amyloidosis). This underscores the regulatory scrutiny on in vivo CRISPR therapies and the importance of demonstrating liver safety across LNP-delivered programs.

Note: This hold illustrates the interconnected nature of safety assessments across CRISPR programs using similar delivery platforms.

Global Trial Landscape

According to CRISPR Medicine News monitoring, 239 clinical trials involving CRISPR technologies are being tracked globally, with Phase 3 trials leading in:

  • Sickle Cell Disease (SCD) and Transfusion-Dependent Beta-Thalassemia (TDT)
  • Transthyretin (ATTR) Amyloidosis
  • Hereditary Angioedema (HAE)

Regulatory & Patent Developments

UC Berkeley vs. Broad Institute Patent Battle:

The U.S. Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit (CAFC) vacated the Patent Trial and Appeal Board (PTAB) ruling on the conception of CRISPR-Cas9. This development reintroduces uncertainty around the foundational CRISPR-Cas9 patent landscape and could impact licensing dynamics for years to come.

FDA Clinical Hold on NTLA-2002:

The FDA placed a clinical hold on Intellia's NTLA-2002 trial for hereditary angioedema following liver safety observations. This regulatory action highlights the heightened scrutiny on in vivo CRISPR therapies, particularly those using lipid nanoparticle delivery to the liver.

EU Regulations on Gene-Edited Crops:

The European Union continues to develop regulatory frameworks for gene-edited agricultural products, with ongoing debates about distinguishing CRISPR edits from traditional GMO classifications.

Developer: Stanford Medicine

AI Applications in CRISPR Workflow

Historic First Personalized Therapy for Infant with CPS1 Mutation
Parameter Details
Patient Baby KJ, infant with carbamoyl phosphate synthetase 1 (CPS1) deficiency
Condition Rare genetic disorder causing life-threatening ammonia buildup
Timeline Therapy designed in 8 months; dosed February-March 2025
Approach Personalized CRISPR-Cas9 therapy targeting patient's specific mutation
Outcome Patient can now tolerate higher dietary protein levels; significant clinical improvement
Significance World's first personalized CRISPR therapy - demonstrates feasibility of n=1 gene therapies for ultra-rare diseases

PERT System: Treating Nonsense Mutations

Technology Breakthrough PERT System (Prime Editing-Mediated Readthrough)

Developer: Broad Institute (David Liu Lab)

Innovation: Prime editing system designed to treat premature termination codons (nonsense mutations)

Mechanism: Uses prime editing to enable ribosomal readthrough of stop codons, restoring full-length protein production

Target Population: Could address approximately 30% of rare genetic diseases caused by nonsense mutations

Significance: Expands the treatable landscape of CRISPR therapies beyond point mutations to include the most severe class of truncating mutations

LNP Delivery Advances

Lipid Nanoparticle Delivery for CFTR Editing

Target: CFTR G542X mutation (cystic fibrosis)

Results from Science Advances Study:

  • 3.5% gene integration efficiency for precise correction
  • 80% correction of CFTR function in bronchial epithelial cells
  • Demonstrated delivery to lung cells via optimized LNP formulations

Significance: Advances LNP technology beyond liver targeting to enable treatment of pulmonary diseases

RIDE VLP Delivery System

RIDE (Virus-Like Particle) System for Cell-Specific Delivery

Innovation: Virus-like particle (VLP) platform for delivering CRISPR ribonucleoproteins (RNPs)

Advantages:

  • Transient delivery - reduces off-target effects
  • Cell-type specific targeting capabilities
  • Non-viral and non-integrating approach
  • Bypasses immune response associated with viral vectors

Applications: Particularly promising for ex vivo editing of therapeutic cell types including CAR-T cells and hematopoietic stem cells

Large-Scale DNA Engineering

Emerging Technologies for Large DNA Insertions:

  • Bridge Recombinases: Enzymes that facilitate large DNA insertions without double-strand breaks, enabling safer integration of therapeutic cassettes
  • evoCAST Systems: Evolved CRISPR-associated transposases for programmable DNA integration
  • CAST Systems: CRISPR-associated transposases enabling large-scale DNA engineering with improved precision

Impact: These technologies address one of CRISPR's major limitations - the inability to efficiently insert large DNA sequences - opening doors to treating diseases requiring gene replacement rather than editing
