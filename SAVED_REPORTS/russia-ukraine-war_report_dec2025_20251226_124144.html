Russia-Ukraine War: Weekly Developments Report

December 20-26, 2025 | Day 1,395-1,401 of the Full-Scale Invasion

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: This week witnessed intensified hostilities over Christmas alongside accelerated diplomatic efforts. Ukrainian forces struck major Russian oil infrastructure using British Storm Shadow missiles, while Russian drone and missile attacks killed at least 11 civilians across Ukraine. Peace negotiations advanced with the release of a 20-point US-Ukrainian proposal, though significant obstacles remain.

Major Offensive Operations

Ukrainian Strikes on Russian Infrastructure

Ukraine executed several successful long-range strikes against Russian energy and military infrastructure:

  • Novoshakhtinsk Oil Refinery: Ukrainian forces used British-supplied Storm Shadow missiles to strike this major refinery in Russia's Rostov region. The Ukrainian General Staff reported "numerous explosions" and noted the facility directly supplies Russian military operations.
  • Temryuk Port: Ukrainian drones targeted oil storage facilities, causing a massive fire across roughly 2,000 square meters (21,500 square feet).
  • Tamanneftegaz Terminal: Strikes damaged pipeline infrastructure, two berths, two vessels, and caused a fire exceeding 1,000 square meters.
  • Yefremov Synthetic Rubber Plant: Located in Tula Oblast, this facility produces components for explosives and solid rocket fuel.
  • Crimean Naval Drone Storage: Ukrainian forces struck a Russian storage and maintenance site for naval drones near occupied Myrnyi, Crimea.

Russian Gains and Territorial Changes

Weekly Territorial Changes:

  • December 16-22: Russia gained 51 square miles of Ukrainian territory
  • November 25 - December 22: Russia gained 138 square miles total
  • 2025 average monthly Russian gains: 176 square miles

Key Russian Advances:

  • Siversk: Russian forces likely completed seizure of this town (pre-war population under 11,000) after 41 months of fighting. Russian forces advanced to heights west and northwest of the town and reached the chalk quarry.
  • Hrabovske: Russian forces entered this border village in Sumy Oblast, part of new cross-border operations.
  • Svyato-Pokrovske: Russia's Defense Ministry claimed control of this settlement in Donetsk region.

Ukrainian Counteroffensive Successes:

  • Kupyansk Direction: Ukrainian forces liberated significant portions of Kupyansk that Russian forces had falsely claimed to capture in late November. Geolocated footage shows Ukrainian forces advanced along the P-79 highway and encircled up to two understrength Russian companies. Russian milbloggers acknowledged Ukrainian gains of approximately 182.64 square kilometers in the Kupyansk direction since December 11.
  • Pokrovsk Direction: Ukrainian forces recently advanced along the E-50 highway and in northwestern Pokrovsk, with Russian infiltrations not changing frontline control.

Diplomatic Developments

20-Point Peace Plan Released

President Zelensky revealed the latest US-Ukrainian-European 20-point peace plan on December 23, marking a significant evolution from the previous 28-point proposal. Key differences include:

  • Territorial Provisions: The plan calls for freezing the frontlines along all four occupied oblasts (Luhansk, Donetsk, Zaporizhia, and Kherson) as of the agreement signing date, rather than just Zaporizhia and Kherson.
  • Military Cap: Ukraine's peacetime military cap increased from 600,000 to 800,000 personnel.
  • NATO: No explicit prohibition on Ukrainian NATO membership (references may be in unpublished security guarantee documents).

Unresolved Issues:

  • Zaporizhzhia Nuclear Power Plant: Control mechanisms remain contested. The US proposal reportedly involved joint US-Ukraine-Russia management, which Zelensky rejected as "very inappropriate."
  • Donetsk Oblast: Zelensky proposed demilitarizing or creating a free economic zone in Ukrainian-held portions of Donetsk, with withdrawal subject to a referendum. Russia demands complete Ukrainian withdrawal from all Donetsk.
  • Security Guarantees: Technical details about enforcement mechanisms and monitoring remain under discussion.

Russian Position and Obstacles

The Kremlin has signaled it will reject key elements of the proposal. Russian officials stated that proposals from Ukrainian and European delegations are "rather unconstructive" and do not "improve" the settlement agreement. Key Russian demands from Putin's June 2024 speech remain:

  • Complete Ukrainian withdrawal from Luhansk, Donetsk, Zaporizhia, and Kherson oblasts
  • Ukrainian abandonment of NATO aspirations and commitment to neutrality
  • Demilitarization and "denazification" (regime change)
  • International recognition of Russia's annexations
  • Lifting of all Western sanctions

Russia reportedly rejected a US-proposed Christmas ceasefire. Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Sergei Ryabkov stated Russia does not support a temporary ceasefire and called only for a permanent ceasefire as part of an agreement eliminating "root causes."

Miami Talks

Separate US meetings with Ukrainian and Russian delegations occurred in Miami on December 19-21:

  • US Special Envoy Steve Witkoff and former Senior Advisor Jared Kushner met Ukrainian Defense Council Secretary Rustem Umerov and Chief of General Staff Major General Andriy Hnatov.
  • Russian negotiator Kirill Dmitriev held separate meetings with the US team.
  • European representatives from Germany, France, and the UK participated in Ukrainian-US talks.

Humanitarian Impact and Civilian Casualties

Christmas Attacks

Both sides conducted strikes during the Christmas period, resulting in civilian deaths:

  • December 24 Russian Attack: A massive combined drone and missile attack targeted 13 Ukrainian regions with 650 drones and 30 missiles, killing at least 3 people including a 4-year-old girl in Zhytomyr region.
  • Zaporizhzhia Region: Russian forces launched 705 strikes on 25 settlements between December 24-25, injuring five people in the city and damaging hundreds of buildings and vehicles.
  • Kharkiv TPP Strike: Russian forces struck a thermal power plant in Kharkiv City suburbs, killing at least one person and injuring 13. The strike disrupted power generation, heat supplies, and transport.
  • Odesa Port Infrastructure: A sunflower oil spill from tanks damaged by Russian bombardments contaminated the shoreline around Odesa, killing wildlife and forcing temporary port closure.
  • Moscow Car Bombing: An explosion in Moscow killed three people, including two police officers. Ukrainian military intelligence (GUR) stated this was a Ukrainian operation targeting officers involved in the war.

Energy Infrastructure Attacks

Russia continues its campaign against Ukrainian energy infrastructure:

  • Emergency power outages introduced across Rivne, Ternopil, and other regions
  • Ukrainian state energy operator Ukrenergo reported outages in Sumy, Dnipropetrovsk, Kherson, Kharkiv, and Chernihiv oblasts
  • Russian strikes particularly targeted critical infrastructure in Chernihiv Oblast on December 23-24
  • Ukrnafta reported nearly 100 Russian strike drones against its production facilities between December 22-24

Forced Deportations

Russian forces forcibly deported approximately 50 civilians from Hrabovske, Sumy Oblast, according to Mayor Larysa Kremenza. Most of those deported were elderly residents who had previously refused evacuation. Forced deportation of residents from occupied territories across international borders is illegal under the Fourth Geneva Convention.

Military Analysis

Putin's Cognitive Warfare Campaign

Analysts have identified a pattern of false and exaggerated reporting influencing Russian President Vladimir Putin's understanding of the battlefield:

  • Exaggerated Claims: Putin claimed Russian forces seized Siversk, Vovchansk, 50% of Lyman, and more than half of Kostyantynivka. ISW found no evidence to confirm these claims, with Russian milblogger claims showing only 7-11% control.
  • Kupyansk Deception: Russian military officials falsely claimed complete seizure of Kupyansk in late November. Putin attempted to discredit geolocated footage of Zelensky's December 12 visit to the town as fake.
  • Source of Reports: Russian Chief of General Staff Valery Gerasimov briefs Putin regularly. Financial Times reported that military officials provide updates that inflate Ukrainian casualties, highlight Russian resource advantages, and downplay failures.
  • Economic Reality: While Putin claims the Russian economy can sustain the war, Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent estimated actual inflation over 20%, and Russia has begun selling gold reserves to fund the state budget.

Russian Tactical Developments

  • Cross-Border Attacks: Russian forces conducted limited cross-border attacks in Sumy and Kharkiv oblasts, including seizure of Hrabovske. ISW assesses these are likely cognitive warfare operations designed to create the appearance of a collapsing Ukrainian front rather than genuine offensive capabilities.
  • Drone Stockpiles: Ukraine estimates Russia has stockpiled approximately 2,000 strike drones, including about 1,400 Shahed drones, for winter operations.
  • Targeting Tactics: Russian forces are using modified Shahed drones with AI capabilities to target moving objects, including locomotives and repair crews.
  • Assault Tactics: The Russian assault on Sotnytskyi Kozachok used shoulder-to-shoulder infantry formations inconsistent with typical Russian small-group infiltration tactics, suggesting the attack was designed to be highly visible rather than militarily effective.

Equipment and Personnel Losses

Ukrainian forces inflicted significant losses during Russian assaults:

  • Dobropillya Assault: Ukrainian forces destroyed at least 8 tanks, 18 IFVs, 5 APCs, and 11 motorcycles during a company-sized Russian mechanized assault.
  • Pokrovsk Direction: Ukrainian 7th Rapid Reaction Corps reported 1,243 Russian casualties in December 2025 as of December 22.

International Support and Reactions

European Union

  • EU leaders agreed to provide Ukraine with 90 billion euros ($105 billion) in interest-free loans for 2026-2027
  • France announced continued work on providing Ukraine with solid security guarantees through the "coalition of the willing" starting in January 2026

NATO and Regional Security

  • Poland scrambled fighter jets to intercept a Russian reconnaissance aircraft near Baltic Sea airspace
  • Poland reported dozens of objects entered its airspace from Belarus overnight
  • Two Russian-made reconnaissance drones crashed in Turkey in recent weeks

US Diplomatic Changes

Democratic senators urged President Trump to reverse the recall of nearly 30 career ambassadors, warning the move creates a leadership vacuum that allows adversaries like Russia and China to expand influence.

Economic Impact

Energy Sector

  • Russian LNG: Russia's target of producing 100 million tonnes of liquefied natural gas annually has been pushed back by several years due to international sanctions.
  • Kazakhstan Oil Exports: CPC Blend oil exports reached their lowest in 14 months in December due to bad weather delaying repair of Russian loading infrastructure after Ukrainian drone strikes.
  • Ukrainian Energy: Russia's strike campaign has destroyed approximately 64% of Ukraine's electricity generating capacity. The grid has been split east-west, with eastern regions "at the brink" of blackout.

Fiscal Developments

  • Ukraine completed settlement of a deal to restructure $2.6 billion of growth-linked debt
  • Ukrainian F-16 pilots shot down 621 of 673 Russian aerial targets during the December 23 massive attack

Key Takeaways and Outlook

  • Peace Prospects Uncertain: While the 20-point plan represents progress, Russia's repeated references to Putin's June 2024 maximalist demands suggest the Kremlin will reject compromises. The proposal lacks provisions Russia deems essential, including NATO expansion guarantees and sufficient limits on Ukraine's military.
  • Military Stalemate Continues: Russia continues making slow, grinding territorial gains (averaging 176 square miles monthly in 2025), but at high cost. Ukrainian counteroffensives in Kupyansk demonstrate Russian vulnerabilities and the effectiveness of false reporting in degrading Russian military effectiveness.
  • Winter Campaign Intensifying: Both sides are preparing for escalated winter operations. Russia has stockpiled approximately 2,000 strike drones, while Ukraine continues targeting Russian energy infrastructure to degrade Moscow's war capacity.
  • Humanitarian Crisis Deepening: Russian attacks on energy infrastructure will likely cause severe hardship for Ukrainian civilians during winter months, while continued forced deportations from occupied territories constitute ongoing violations of international law.
