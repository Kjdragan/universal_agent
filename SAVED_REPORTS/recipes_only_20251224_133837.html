Marinated Vegetable Recipes

MARINATED ASPARAGUS

Classic Balsamic Marinated Asparagus

Ingredients:
  • 2 pounds fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 1 1/2 cups balsamic vinaigrette
  • 2 teaspoons lemon zest
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Blanch asparagus just until tender, about 1-3 minutes depending on thickness.
  2. Immediately plunge into ice water to stop cooking and preserve color.
  3. Drain asparagus and place in a large resealable bag with the vinaigrette.
  4. Refrigerate at least 3 hours (overnight is even better), turning occasionally.
  5. Just before serving, drain and arrange on a platter. Sprinkle with lemon zest, parsley, salt, and pepper.
Reader Tip: "Don't skip the lemon zest part. It adds to the flavor and a great presentation."
Storage: Keeps 2-3 days refrigerated. Serve cold or at room temperature.

Tangy Marinated Asparagus

Ingredients:
  • 1 pound asparagus, trimmed
  • Marinade:
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped red onion
  • 1 tbsp balsamic, sherry, or red-wine vinegar
  • 1 tsp freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
Cooking Guidance:
  • Thin asparagus: 5-7 minutes boiling
  • Thick asparagus: May need up to 2-3 minutes longer
  • Target texture: Al dente—slightly crisp but not raw
  1. Boil or steam asparagus until al dente (about 7 minutes). Drain and let cool.
  2. Make marinade: In small bowl combine red onion, vinegar, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, salt, pepper, and olive oil.
  3. Toss cooled asparagus with vinaigrette, ensuring each spear is coated.
  4. Transfer to covered container and refrigerate several hours (or overnight).
  5. Let spears come to room temperature before serving.

Asian-Style Marinated Asparagus

Ingredients:
  • 1-2 pounds asparagus, trimmed
  • Marinade:
  • Soy sauce
  • Sesame oil
  • Rice vinegar
  1. Sauté asparagus in oil for 2-3 minutes per batch (single layer) until crisp-tender.
  2. Whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, and rice vinegar to taste.
  3. Toss warm asparagus with marinade.
  4. Let sit at room temperature for 15 minutes, then refrigerate at least 2 hours.
  5. Serve cold or at room temperature.

Soy-Glazed Marinated Asparagus

Ingredients:
  • 1 pound asparagus, trimmed
  • Marinade:
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  1. Simmer asparagus for 3-5 minutes until tender-crisp.
  2. Rinse immediately in cold water and drain.
  3. Mix brown sugar, vinegar, soy sauce, oil, lemon juice, and garlic powder.
  4. Pour marinade over cooled asparagus.
  5. Refrigerate at least 3 hours before serving.
Tip: "A nice accompaniment to chicken, roast beef or ham."

MARINATED GREEN BEANS

Italian-Style Marinated Green Beans

Ingredients:
  • 1 pound green beans, trimmed
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red-wine vinegar
  • 1 clove garlic, grated
  • Fresh basil and mint, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Pinch of black pepper
  • Pinch of crushed red pepper (optional)
  1. Bring 2 quarts water with 1/2 tablespoon salt to a boil.
  2. Add green beans and cook for 2 minutes (or 4 minutes for less-crisp texture).
  3. Strain and pat dry with kitchen towels.
  4. Mix olive oil, vinegar, garlic, basil, mint, salt, and pepper in small bowl.
  5. Add still-warm beans and marinade to shallow dish or zip-top bag.
  6. Let sit 15 minutes, then refrigerate at least 2 hours.
  7. Serve cold or at room temperature.
Important: "These beans will lose their bright green color within a few hours of immersion in the vinaigrette. If the green is important to you, limit to 1 hour in vinaigrette before serving."

Mediterranean Marinated Green Beans with Feta

Ingredients:
  • 1 pound green beans, trimmed
  • 1/3 cup Kalamata olives
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped (or cherry/grape tomatoes)
  • 1/3 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red-wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  1. Blanch green beans 3-5 minutes (not 8-10!).
  2. Immediately drain and plunge into ice water.
  3. Heat olive oil in skillet; cook garlic 30 seconds.
  4. Remove from heat; stir in olives, tomatoes, vinegar, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  5. Pour mixture over green beans; toss to coat.
  6. Sprinkle feta on top and chill at least 3 hours before serving.
Popular Variations:
  • Add red onion for additional color and bite
  • Double the marinade for better coverage
  • Add artichoke hearts
  • Add a pinch of sugar to balance the acid

Whole30 Compliant Marinated Green Beans

Ingredients:
  • 1 pound green beans, trimmed
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard (Whole30 compliant)
  • 2 tablespoons red-wine vinegar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. Simmer beans for 3 minutes until crisp-tender.
  2. Drain and plunge into ice water.
  3. Whisk together olive oil, mustard, vinegar, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  4. Toss beans with marinade while still warm.
  5. Refrigerate at least 2 hours before serving.

Sweet and Savory Marinated Green Beans

Ingredients:
  • 1 pound green beans, trimmed
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup vinegar (white or apple cider)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (optional)
  1. Blanch green beans 2-3 minutes until bright green and crisp-tender.
  2. Shock in ice water, drain well.
  3. Mix oil, vinegar, sugar, salt, pepper, and garlic.
  4. Place beans in container, pour marinade over.
  5. Refrigerate 8 hours or overnight for maximum flavor.

Classic Food Network Marinated Green Beans

Ingredients:
  • 1 pound green beans, trimmed
  • 1/3 cup slivered olives
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons capers
  • 3 tablespoons red-wine vinegar
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. Cook beans 8-10 minutes until tender but not mushy.
  2. Drain well and add to bowl with olives, garlic, basil, oregano, and capers.
  3. Add vinegar first, toss well.
  4. Add oil and toss again.
  5. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Let stand at room temperature 3 hours, remove garlic cloves, serve.

PICKLED RED ONIONS WITH JALAPEÑOS

Classic Quick Pickled Red Onions

Ingredients:
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 jalapeño, sliced
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (any type)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  1. Place jalapeño and onions in medium mixing bowl.
  2. Heat vinegar, water, sugar, and salt in small skillet over high heat, whisking until dissolved.
  3. Bring to boil, then pour over onions.
  4. Press onions down with spoon until submerged.
  5. Place double layer of paper towels on top and press down until saturated.
  6. Allow to sit 25 minutes until softened and cooled.
  7. Transfer to sealed container and refrigerate.
Storage: Will keep indefinitely in refrigerator.

Red Wine and Lime Pickled Onions

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup filtered water
  • 1 cup red-wine vinegar
  • 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 1 tbsp honey (or sugar)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 large red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1-2 jalapeños, sliced
  1. Combine water, vinegar, lime juice, honey, and salt in saucepan.
  2. Bring to boil, remove from heat, allow to cool.
  3. Thinly slice onions and jalapeños.
  4. Transfer cooled brine to glass container, add vegetables ensuring submerged.
  5. Refrigerate at least 1 hour before using.
Storage: Keeps 3-4 days refrigerated.

Apple Cider Vinegar Pickled Onions

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1-2 red onions, thinly sliced
  • 1-2 jalapeños, sliced
  • Pickling spice (optional)
  1. Dissolve sugar and salt in warm water.
  2. Add vinegar and mix well.
  3. Place onions and jalapeño in jar.
  4. Pour brine over vegetables.
  5. Add pickling spice if using.
  6. Close lid, shake well.
  7. Let sit on counter 30 minutes, then refrigerate.
Storage: Keeps 3-4 weeks refrigerated.

Garlic and Jalapeño Pickled Onions

Ingredients:
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (any variety)
  • 1 tablespoon sweetener (sugar, honey, maple)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 clove garlic, microplaned
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 jalapeño, thinly sliced
Vinegar Options:
  • White or apple cider: more bite
  • Rice vinegar: milder
  • White or red wine vinegar: slightly sweeter
  1. Microwave vinegar, sweetener, salt, and water 1 minute (or heat on stovetop).
  2. Remove from heat, stir in garlic.
  3. Let brine cool while slicing vegetables.
  4. Divide vegetables into jars.
  5. Pour cooled brine over, covering completely.
  6. Refrigerate at least 24 hours for best flavor.
Storage: Keeps 3 weeks. "The longer they sit the better they get."

Citrus-Pickled Onions

Ingredients:
  • 1 jumbo red onion (or 2 smaller), thinly sliced
  • 1-2 fresh jalapeño peppers, sliced
  • 2 limes (1/3 cup juice)
  • 1 orange (1/3 cup juice)
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp cider vinegar
  1. Blanch onions and jalapeño 30 seconds in boiling water.
  2. Run under cold water immediately.
  3. Mix citrus juices, salt, sugar, and vinegar.
  4. Combine vegetables with juice mixture.
  5. Refrigerate 1-24 hours before serving.
Tip: "Best at 1-2 hours, but still delicious the next day."

Long-Storage Pickled Onions with Cilantro

Ingredients:
  • 2 red onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 jalapeños, sliced
  • 1 cup vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 bunch cilantro stems (leaves saved for other use)
  • 1 teaspoon peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
  1. Combine vinegar, water, sugar, and salt in saucepan.
  2. Bring to boil, stirring until dissolved.
  3. Pack onions, jalapeños, cilantro stems, and spices into jars.
  4. Pour hot brine over vegetables.
  5. Let cool, seal tightly, refrigerate.
Storage: Keeps up to 6 months refrigerated. Can be water-bath canned (10 minutes) for room-temperature storage.
