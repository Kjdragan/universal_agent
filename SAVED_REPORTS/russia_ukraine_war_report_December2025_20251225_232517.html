Peace Negotiations and Diplomatic Developments

The 20-Point Peace Framework

President Volodymyr Zelenskyy revealed details of a comprehensive 20-point peace plan agreed upon by US and Ukrainian negotiators following marathon talks. According to PBS reporting, the plan addresses one of the most contentious issues: the fate of the Donetsk region, which Ukraine still partially holds but Russia has failed to capture despite 11 years of war.

Zelenskyy's Compromise Proposal:

"We consider a free economic zone a potential option for a sovereign state to choose." The proposal would demilitarize or create a free economic zone in the portion of Donetsk that Ukraine currently holds, with Ukrainian military withdrawal conditioned upon reciprocal Russian pullback and international force monitoring.

Key Elements of the Proposal:

  • Ceasefire first, followed by 60 days to prepare referendum
  • Ukrainian police would remain in the proposed free economic zone
  • Only Ukrainians living in Ukrainian territory could vote (excluding those under occupation)
  • International forces must be present on the ground to guarantee security
  • Similar arrangement possible for Zaporizhzhia nuclear power plant area
Major Obstacles Remain:

Moscow has given no indication it will agree to any withdrawal from seized territory. Russia has repeatedly rejected the presence of foreign troops on the front line. Bloomberg reports Russia is seeking changes to the text and that it lacks provisions important to Moscow. Additionally, Zelenskyy rejects US proposals for joint US-Russia management of Zaporizhzhia nuclear plant, calling it "inappropriate and not entirely realistic."

Ukrainian Operations

Ukraine has increasingly employed drone warfare to strike targets deep inside Russian territory:

  • Temryuk Port (Krasnodar): Ukrainian drone strike on two storage tanks holding oil products caused a fire spreading across 2,000 square meters
  • Novoshakhtinsk Oil Refinery (Rostov): Storm Shadow missiles struck this major supplier of oil products for southern Russia
  • Orenburg Gas Processing Plant: Targeted by long-range Ukrainian drones
  • Yefremov Synthetic Rubber Plant (Tula): Struck by Ukrainian drones
  • Marine Drone Storage Facility (Crimea): Targeted by Ukrainian forces
Energy Infrastructure Warfare:

According to Russia Matters, Ukrainian drone strikes have forced nearly 40% of Russia's oil refining capacity offline, with at least 70% of shutdowns directly linked to these strikes. However, Reuters estimates that Russia's oil processing has fallen less than expected despite the attacks. Conversely, Russia has destroyed approximately 64% of Ukraine's electricity generating capacity, with ISW reporting that Russia's strike campaign is "degrading" Ukraine's power grid, with eastern regions "at the brink" of blackout.

Civilian Impact and Humanitarian Consequences

Casualties and Attacks on Civilians

Odesa Port Attack (Dec 19)

Russian ballistic missile attack killed seven people and wounded 15. Governor Oleh Kiper described the attack as "massive," with trucks catching fire.

Moscow Explosion (Dec 24)

Car explosion killed three people including two police officers. Ukraine's GUR claimed it was a Ukrainian operation targeting officers involved in the war.

Dnipro Region Attacks

Artillery shelling and drones damaged homes, power lines, and a gas pipeline.

Environmental Impact

Sunflower Oil Spill in Odesa

A sunflower oil spill caused by Russian aerial bombardments has contaminated the shoreline around Odesa, killing wildlife and triggering warnings from conservationists. Governor Oleh Kiper reported: "The cause was damage to sunflower oil tanks as a result of massive enemy attacks on port infrastructure." The Pivdenny port was temporarily closed for cleanup.

Russian air defense systems remain active against Ukrainian drones:

  • December 24: 172 Ukrainian drones destroyed overnight; 16 drones en route to Moscow repelled over 17 hours; two major Moscow airports forced to limit operations
  • Tula Region: 12 Ukrainian drones destroyed; debris from downed drone ignited fire at industrial site

Incidents Beyond Borders

Poland sent fighter jets to intercept a Russian reconnaissance aircraft near its airspace over the Baltic Sea and reported that dozens of objects entered Polish airspace from Belarus overnight. Polish officials warned these incidents during the holiday season may signal a provocation.

Turkiye found a Russian-made Orlan-10 reconnaissance drone in the Izmit district, after previously shooting down a drone over the Black Sea. Turkiye informed both Kyiv and Moscow "of the need to act cautiously" to avoid negatively affecting security in the Black Sea.

Factors Shaping Expectations

Military Factors

Russian military advances on the battlefield continue, with gradual territorial gains contributing to public confidence.

Geopolitical Factors

Washington's reluctance to continue financing the war and EU's inability to fully replace US support create expectations of a settlement favoring Russia.

Ukrainian Position

Zelenskyy's peace plan announcement represents a significant shift in public positioning, acknowledging that territorial compromises may be necessary to end the war. The requirement for a referendum places the ultimate decision in Ukrainian citizens' hands, though the president has framed this starkly as "either this or war."

International Energy Impacts

Kazakhstan Oil Exports Hit 14-Month Low

Kazakhstan's CPC Blend oil exports reached their lowest level in 14 months in December, as bad weather delays repairs to Russian loading infrastructure after Ukrainian drone strikes. On November 29, Ukrainian drones hit the Caspian Pipeline Consortium terminal near Novorossiysk, leaving just one of three jetties operational.

Russia's Deputy Prime Minister Alexander Novak announced that Russia's target of producing 100 million tonnes of liquefied natural gas annually has been pushed back by several years due to international sanctions.

Financial Developments

Ukraine's Ministry of Finance completed settlement of a deal to restructure $2.6 billion of growth-linked debt. EU leaders agreed to provide funding to meet Ukraine's military and economic needs for the next two years, deciding to borrow cash on capital markets rather than use frozen Russian assets.

Assessment and Outlook

Current Strategic Balance

The military situation shows Russia making gradual territorial advances at a sustainable pace, averaging 176 square miles per month in 2025. However, these gains come at high cost and Ukrainian forces continue to demonstrate capability to strike deep into Russian territory with drones and missiles.

Diplomatic Prospects

Positive Indicators

  • US-led diplomatic process producing concrete frameworks
  • Both sides engaged in discussions through intermediaries
  • Zelenskyy offering territorial concessions previously off the table
  • Russian public expectation of 2026 war end

Major Obstacles

  • Russia has not agreed to any withdrawal from seized territory
  • Moscow rejects international peacekeeping forces on front lines
  • Zaporizhzhia nuclear plant control remains unresolved
  • Referendum requirement creates uncertainty

Key Uncertainties

  • Whether Putin will accept any compromise less than full control of Donbas
  • If Ukrainian public would support territorial concessions in a referendum
  • What security guarantees would be sufficient for Ukraine
  • How the Trump administration's personnel changes affect policy
  • Whether military dynamics will shift significantly in early 2026

Conclusion

December 2025 has revealed both the possibilities and limitations of diplomatic initiatives to end the Russia-Ukraine war. While a concrete peace framework exists and high-level discussions are occurring, fundamental disagreements on territory, security guarantees, and the future of occupied regions remain unresolved. The gradual pace of Russian advances continues while Ukraine demonstrates an ability to project force into Russian territory. The coming weeks and months will test whether diplomatic momentum can overcome these entrenched positions.
