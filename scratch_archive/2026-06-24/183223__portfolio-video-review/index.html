The question isn't HyperFrames vs NotebookLM. It's brand-matched craft vs rapid style variety — and the portfolio's job is to showcase craft.
recommendation

Primary case-study video: HyperFrames, brand-matched. It's on-palette, reliable (deterministic render, can't flake), and visibly produced by the pipeline the portfolio sells. Use the SonosKD and Deep Research brand-matched renders as the embedded demos.

NotebookLM's role: a "we can also generate these" option. Its strength is rapid style variety (watercolor, anime, etc.) — genuinely useful for audience-testing or varied client brands, weaker as the primary portfolio asset because it isn't visibly yours. Mention it in the HyperFrames case study; don't embed it as the demo.

Open capability bet: if the live-video-as-HyperFrames-asset experiment lands, it closes the gap with screen-tracer footage — real recording + authored polish. Don't count on it yet, but the case study framing is flexible enough to slot it in as an evolution.
