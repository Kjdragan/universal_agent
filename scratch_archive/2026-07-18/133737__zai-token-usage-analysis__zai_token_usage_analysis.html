ZAI Token Usage — Who Burns the Weekly Budget

Read-only analysis · 2026-07-18 · window analyzed: Jul 11 – Jul 17 (the week that exhausted the ZAI weekly limit; reset 2026-07-19 00:54 Beijing / Jul 18 11:54 AM Houston). Nothing was changed — findings and recommendations only.

TL;DR. The measurement wheel already exists and is production-wired — four capture lanes, a consolidated dashboard, an AIMD rate limiter, and an L0–L4 emergency control plane. What it was built for is burst/429 management, not a weekly budget. Measured for the limit week: ~489M total ZAI tokens — ~127M input+output plus ~362M cache-read. The top burners are the glm-5.2 principal sessions (VP missions + Simone heartbeats, dominated by cache reads) and two Mission-Control dashboard functions that regenerate ~100–150k-token syntheses around the clock (~76M/week, watched or not). The sharpest defect: the new weekly-limit error code 1310 is not recognized by the rate limiter, so the whole stack retried into a dead account for ~2 days until the manual pause.

4 · Waste findings

W1 · The weekly-limit error (code 1310) is invisible to the rate limiter — the stack retried into a dead account for ~2 days.
rate_limiter.py::FUP_KEYWORDS matches "weekly limit" and "1313", but the actual error body is [1310][Weekly/Monthly Limit Exhausted…] — the substring "weekly/monthly limit" does not contain "weekly limit", and 1310 ≠ 1313. Verified in code today. Consequence, observed live: after exhaustion on Jul 17, every scheduled job kept firing and running its full 5-attempt retry ladder (e.g. the YouTube digest burned 5 retries at 11:16 on Jul 18; demo-triage again at 15:05) until the stack was manually paused. The L4 global-pause machinery existed the whole time — nothing was wired to pull the lever.
W2 · Mission-Control regenerates ~76M tokens/week of dashboard intelligence nobody may be looking at.
The sweeper (universal-agent-mission-control-sweeper.service, 60s tick) nets out to a ~104k-token chief-of-staff readout every ~15 minutes and a ~149k-token tier-1 card discovery every ~30 minutes, 24/7. It is deliberately on the mid lane (glm-4.7) and self-throttled — but generation is unconditional on viewership. This is the largest discretionary line item after the principals themselves.
W3 · Cache-read mass is the real budget: ~362M/week (74% of everything).
Every glm-5.2 principal turn re-reads its full cached context (system prompt + HEARTBEAT.md + tool defs + briefing). If ZAI counts cache reads against the weekly cap at or near full weight — the June "~259M/day gap vs the z.ai bill" analysis suggests the bill counts everything — then context size × turn count is the #1 lever, not model choice. Simone's 45KB HEARTBEAT.md and force_complex=True (always load all tools) ride along on all 116 weekly turns.
W4 · Thinking-mode traps on the opus tier (glm-5.2) — silent multiplier risk.
glm-5.2 defaults thinking ON (10–24× tokens per the tiering doc); glm-5.1 had none. Three call sites still carry "GLM-5.1 has no thinking; do NOT pass thinking" comments while now resolving to glm-5.2 with no cap: csi_intelligence_pass.py (3×/day, one opus call per action), the convergence ideation stage, and the YouTube digest reduce step. The /goal evaluator also runs glm-5.2-with-thinking after every Cody turn (documented as an accepted tradeoff; pinnable via UA_GOAL_EVAL_MODEL). Caveat: measured SDK output tokens are modest (~15k/turn), so thinking is not visibly exploding reported output — but whether z.ai bills thinking tokens beyond what usage reports is exactly the kind of thing the weekly cap made expensive. Verify against the z.ai console before and after capping.
W5 · 25% of all direct API calls got 429'd — overlap pressure is self-inflicted.
~840 of 3,301 httpx calls were rejected. The hourly jobs cluster at :00 (csi_convergence_sync, hourly digest) and the Discord relevance filter fired 1,011 times/week (22% rejected). Retries multiply the effective call volume of the heavy callers (chief-of-staff: 171 rejections at ~104k tokens per eventual success). The ZAI plan is also documented single-concurrent-session — the system runs many overlapping consumers against it by design.
W6 · Morning digest pile-up and re-enumeration.
06:00 YouTube digest, 06:30 morning briefing, 07:05 proactive report, 08:30 backlog triage, 08:35 artifact digest — five LLM products in a 2.5h window with overlapping content; the evening briefing re-enumerates every hourly insight already delivered. Token-modest individually (~11M/week for the whole tail) but a consolidation candidate for both cost and signal quality.
W7 · No budget concept anywhere.
The system can measure (4 lanes), report (dashboard), and stop (L0–L4) — but nothing tracks "tokens spent this week vs the weekly cap" or slows down as the budget depletes. The weekly limit was discovered by hitting it.

5 · Recommendations — ranked by savings ÷ effort nothing changed yet

Everything below is wiring/config on top of existing machinery — no new subsystems.

R1 · Teach the stack what 1310 means (small effort, prevents the catastrophic mode). Add "1310" and "limit exhausted" to the detection path, but classify it as exhaustion, not FUP: on sighting, auto-set the existing L4 global pause with a TTL parsed from the reset timestamp in the error body (it says exactly when the limit resets), alert once, and let the pause expire on its own. One keyword-set + one handler; the L4 lever and TTL support already exist in zai_control.py. This converts "2 days of futile retries + manual pause + manual revival" into "self-parking until reset."
R2 · Put Mission-Control intelligence on a viewership or delta gate (config-level, ~40–70M/week back). Options in ascending effort: raise the readout floor (15 min → 60 min saves ~35M/week alone); skip synthesis when the evidence signature hasn't changed; or generate on-demand when the dashboard is actually open (the sweeper already has floor/ceiling knobs via UA_MISSION_CONTROL_SWEEPER_INTERVAL_S and adaptive gating). Also worth trimming the 104–149k evidence payloads.
R3 · Build the weekly budget meter on the existing lanes (the one genuinely new thing). A small rollup — the four lanes are already consolidated by token_consolidation.py — tracking week-to-date total vs a configured cap, exposed on the ZAI-Control dashboard, and wired to auto-escalate the existing control levels (e.g. L1 at 70%, L2 at 85%, cheap-only L3 at 95%). This closes the exact gap all the June work missed.
R4 · Context diet for the principals (biggest absolute lever, medium effort). 360M cache-read/week is mostly re-read context. Candidates: slim HEARTBEAT.md (45KB → a directive index that lazy-loads sections), drop force_complex=True on heartbeat ticks that only triage, and audit VP mission prompts for boilerplate. Even 25% context reduction ≈ 90M tokens/week.
R5 · Thinking hygiene on the opus call sites (small effort, unknown-but-possibly-large savings). Fix the three stale "5.1 has no thinking" call sites to pass an explicit thinking budget (or disable where it's classification, not reasoning), and consider pinning UA_GOAL_EVAL_MODEL to glm-5-turbo now that the 600s-hang issue it dodged may be resolved. Measure the z.ai console delta for a week before/after.
R6 · De-cluster and de-noise the cheap tier (small effort, mostly 429 relief). Offset the :00 hourly jobs by a few minutes each; batch the Discord relevance filter (1,011 single-item calls → a few dozen batched ones — the batched_judge pattern already exists); drop retry ceilings for sub-1k-token classifiers from 5 to 2.

7 · Open questions

  • Does the weekly cap count cache-read tokens, and at what weight? This single fact decides whether R4 (context diet) or R2 (Mission-Control) is the top savings lever. The June billing-gap analysis suggests cache reads count; the z.ai console after a few post-reset days will confirm.
  • What is the actual weekly cap? The 1310 error gives only the reset time. If ~489M observable was the whole week and it exhausted mid-Thursday, the cap is plausibly in the 400–500M range — worth confirming from the z.ai plan page so the R3 meter has a real denominator.
  • Do thinking tokens bill beyond reported output? Reported outputs are modest; the multiplier may be latent. A/B one capped week (R5) answers it.
