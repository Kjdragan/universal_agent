UA · Non-Deterministic Evaluation

Which approach actually cracks non-deterministic evaluation?

Comparing the three lineages we have — the loop_experiments probe, the older URW / Ralph wrapper, and QLoop — against the four pillars a trustworthy subjective-quality judge must stand on.

Verdict: QLoop's methodology wins — decisively. The reason matters more than the result: in non-deterministic evaluation the guards are the product. "Simpler" doesn't mean leaner-but-equivalent; it means missing the specific guards that keep a judge from rubber-stamping. Strip them and you don't get a lighter evaluator — you get a confident, wrong one.

The four pillars — each prevents a specific failure

1Externalize the contract before generating

Write acceptance criteria as a separate explicit spec, not recited inline. Our biggest measured lever: 0.31 → 0.75 at ~1 extra call.

Prevents: a vague target the model silently reinterprets to suit itself.

2Judge evidence, not narration

The judge scores the artifact — rendered output, screenshots, extracted DOM — never the generator's "I'm done" story.

Prevents: the sycophancy hole that sinks bare /loop and /goal.

3Separate scoring from deciding

The LLM judge only scores; a deterministic controller owns accept / refine / stop. The judge can never force an accept.

Prevents: the agreeable model that did the work also deciding it's done — the rubber-stamp itself.

4Validate the judge against human-anchored tiers

Measure whether the judge can tell good from great — per-tier drift, not a pooled average that hides the collapse.

Prevents: a judge silently degraded into a rubber-stamp you can't even see.

What each one is really for

Adopt — the engine

QLoop

The only one that treats the judge as something to be validated, not trusted. That's the conceptual leap — everything else is a fancier way of asking an agreeable model "are you happy?"

→ Adopt its core, not the kitchen sink.
Keep — the proof

loop-it

Its value isn't as an evaluator — it's the empirical evidence for why the pillars work (the 0.31 → 0.75 → 0.875 ladder).

→ Keep as the validation harness.
Salvage — one part

URW / Ralph

Right instinct on cheap deterministic pre-gates (binary/constraint checks before you pay for an LLM judge). Its simplicity lands exactly on the rubber-stamp.

→ Parts donor, not a base.
The honest ceiling: no architecture cracks this alone. QLoop carries the same unsolved residual as the others — its anchors are synthetic. The real crack is QLoop's machinery fed by human-scored anchors: per-tier drift and the discrimination gate are exactly what stop a pooled average from washing out the signal. Without that machinery, even good anchors get averaged into noise; without anchors, even QLoop's machinery is just plumbing. QLoop is the half that's already built — a few dozen human-scored weak→strong exemplars is the half we still owe, and the single highest-leverage thing to produce.
Bottom line: adopt QLoop's four-pillar methodology and its core (the decide controller + the contract + the evidence boundary + the calibration gate). Defer the heavier optional layers (browser-evidence collection, lesson-learning, negotiation) until they earn their place.  ·  Decision recap for UA · 2026-06-22
