## 2. Causes and Motivations

### Primary Causes
1. **Opposition to ICE Operations**
   - Protest against expanded federal Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) operations in Minnesota
   - Response to Operation Metro Surge - large-scale ICE deployment initiated in late 2025
   - Opposition to mass deportation policies of the Trump administration

2. **Fatal Shootings by Federal Agents**
   - **Alex Pretti Shooting (January 24, 2026)**: 37-year-old ICU nurse shot and killed by federal immigration agents in Minneapolis
   - This was the second shooting of a U.S. citizen by federal agents in Minnesota during January 2026
   - First fatal shooting earlier in January 2026: Renee Good (reported by Britannica)

3. **Community Defense Networks**
   - Minneapolis community organizing to warn immigrants of approaching ICE agents
   - Creation of observation networks to monitor federal immigration operations
   - Community-led efforts to document and resist enforcement actions

### Secondary Motivations
- Concerns about excessive use of force by federal agents
- Opposition to what protesters view as overreach in immigration enforcement
- Solidarity with immigrant communities facing increased enforcement
- Defense of civil liberties and due process rights

## 5. Official Responses and Statements

### State Government Response

**Governor Tim Walz**
- **Action**: Mobilized Minnesota National Guard on January 18, 2026
- **Scope**: 1,500 soldiers placed on standby
- **Purpose**: Support local law enforcement, ensure public safety
- **Deployment Status**: As of January 24, 2026, Guard mobilized but not deployed to streets
- **Statement on Church Protests**: Governor stated he does not support protesters interrupting places of worship

**City of Minneapolis**
- **Action**: Formally requested National Guard support on January 24, 2026
- **Justification**: Staffing needs after third shooting by federal agents
- **Recommendation**: Came from Minneapolis Chief of Police
- **Approach**: Guard to support, not replace local law enforcement

### Federal Government Response

**Border Czar Tom Homan**
- **January 30, 2026**: Ordered federal immigration authorities to create drawdown plan for Minnesota law enforcement
- **Context**: Response to ongoing protests and public pressure
- **Significance**: Potential de-escalation of federal enforcement presence

**Department of Justice/Attorney General Pam Bondi**
- **Action**: Charged woman who led anti-immigration enforcement church protest
- **Journalist Don Lemon**: Initially not charged during initial incident, but arrested by January 30, 2026
- **Stance**: Enforcement of federal laws regarding disruption of religious services

**ICE/Federal Immigration Authorities**
- **Personnel Actions**: Agents involved in Alex Pretti shooting placed on leave (January 28, 2026)
- **Policy Changes**: Reported new orders to ease tactics (January 29, 2026)
- **Drawdown Plan**: Ordered to develop plan for reducing Minnesota operations

### Law Enforcement Response

**Minneapolis Police Department**
- Requested National Guard support for staffing

- Chief of Police recommended Guard deployment

**Federal Agents**
- Multiple shootings in January 2026:
