# Minnesota Protests: Executive Summary
## January 27-30, 2026

---

## QUICK OVERVIEW

**What**: Large-scale anti-ICE protests across Minnesota opposing federal immigration enforcement  
**When**: January 27-30, 2026 (continuing from earlier January protests)  
**Where**: Primarily Minneapolis-St. Paul, with statewide participation  
**Who**: Thousands of protesters, labor unions, faith communities, students, immigrant rights advocates  
**Why**: Response to fatal shootings by federal agents and expanded ICE operations

- Student walkouts in multiple cities

## PRIMARY CAUSES

### 1. Fatal Shootings by Federal Agents
- **Alex Pretti (January 24)**: 37-year-old VA ICU nurse shot and killed
- **Renee Good (Earlier January)**: First fatal shooting of month
- Both were U.S. citizens
- Agents involved in Pretti shooting placed on leave (January 28)

### 2. Expanded ICE Operations
- **Operation Metro Surge**: Large-scale ICE deployment initiated late 2025
- 2,000+ additional immigration officers deployed to Minnesota
- Mass deportation policies opposed by protesters

### 3. Community Resistance
- Networks formed to warn immigrants of ICE operations
- Observation teams monitoring federal agents
- Documentation of enforcement actions

## OFFICIAL RESPONSES

### State Government
**Governor Tim Walz**
- Mobilized **1,500 National Guard soldiers** (January 18)
- Placed on standby for potential deployment
- As of Jan 24: Mobilized but not deployed to streets
- Stated opposition to protesters disrupting places of worship

**City of Minneapolis**
- Formally requested National Guard support (January 24)
- Reason: Staffing needs after third shooting by federal agents
- Guard to support, not replace local law enforcement

### Federal Government
**Border Czar Tom Homan**
- Ordered drawdown plan for Minnesota (January 30)
- Potential de-escalation of federal presence

**Attorney General Pam Bondi**
- Charged leaders of church disruption protest
- Federal arrest of journalist Don Lemon by January 30

**ICE/Federal Authorities**
- Agents in Pretti shooting placed on administrative leave
- New orders reported to ease tactics (January 29)
- Under Department of Justice investigation
