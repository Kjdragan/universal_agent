Foundation Model Funding Dominance

Foundation model companies raised $80 billion in 2025, representing 40% of all AI funding—more than doubling from $31 billion in 2024. OpenAI and Anthropic alone captured 14% of global venture investment. The largest deal was SoftBank's $40 billion investment into OpenAI, marking it as the most valuable private company ever at $500 billion valuation.

Geographic Concentration

The United States dominated AI funding with $159 billion (79% of total), with the San Francisco Bay Area alone accounting for $122 billion—more than three-quarters of U.S. AI funding. This concentration reflects the continued dominance of U.S. AI labs and infrastructure providers.

Investor Shift: Private Equity Dominance

Private equity and alternative investors led the largest amounts in 2025, with PE-led deals totaling $63 billion across approximately 300 rounds. By contrast, venture capital firms led $38 billion across 1,600 fundings. While VC was more active (leading 75% of deals), PE dominated the mega-rounds above $1 billion.

Key Insight: 58% of AI funding was in "megarounds" of $500 million or more, indicating capital concentration at the top of the market. The three venture firms leading billion-dollar AI deals were Lightspeed Venture Partners, Founders Fund, and Andreessen Horowitz.

Build vs. Buy: The Pivot to Purchasing

In a significant shift from 2024, enterprises now purchase 76% of AI solutions rather than building them internally (up from 53% in 2024). This reflects the maturation of ready-made AI solutions that demonstrate immediate value. Companies are prioritizing rapid deployment over custom builds, accelerating time-to-production.

AI Deal Conversion Rates

AI buyers convert at nearly twice the rate of traditional software: 47% of AI deals go to production compared to 25% for traditional SaaS. This elevated conversion reflects strong buyer commitment and clear immediate value. Organizations surface an average of 10+ potential AI use cases but focus adoption on near-term productivity gains.

Product-Led Growth Dominance

Individual users drive AI adoption at 4x the rate of traditional software (27% vs. 7% PLG penetration). When accounting for "shadow AI" (employees using personal credit cards for tools like ChatGPT Plus), PLG-driven tools may represent close to 40% of application AI spend. Tools like Cursor reached $200 million in revenue before hiring a single enterprise sales rep.

Startup vs. Incumbent Performance

AI-native startups captured nearly $2 in revenue for every $1 earned by incumbents—63% of the application market, up from 36% last year. This dominance is strongest in agile departments like market research, sales, marketing, and product. Incumbents maintain stronger positions in IT and data science, where reliability and deep integrations outweigh speed.

Anthropic's Ascent

In a decisive shift, Anthropic unseated OpenAI as the enterprise leader, growing from 24% market share in 2024 to 40% in 2025. Over the same period, OpenAI fell from 50% to 27%. Google also gained significantly, increasing from 7% in 2023 to 21% in 2025. Together, these three companies account for 88% of enterprise LLM API usage.

The Coding Battlefield

Anthropic's dominance is driven by its position in the coding market, where it commands 54% market share compared to 21% for OpenAI. This is up from 42% just six months ago, driven by Claude Code's popularity. Anthropic has maintained an 18-month lead atop LLM leaderboards for coding, starting with Claude Sonnet 3.5 in June 2024.

Critical Development: When Google released Gemini 3 Pro in mid-November 2025, its model card showed it leading most major evaluations—except SWE-bench Verified, where it still trailed Claude Sonnet 4.5. Just a week later, Anthropic widened the gap again with Claude Opus 4.5, resetting the high-water mark for code generation.

ChatGPT's Sustained Dominance

ChatGPT maintains massive consumer adoption with an estimated 800-900 million weekly active users across platforms. Its DAU/MAU ratio of 36% is nearly double Gemini's 21%, and month-12 desktop user retention of 50% is similarly double Gemini's 25%. However, Gemini is growing faster—155% YoY on desktop compared to ChatGPT's 23%.

Paid User Growth

Both platforms show strong paid growth. Gemini is growing Pro subscriptions nearly 300% YoY compared to 155% for ChatGPT. Paid user retention is similar—at 68% by month 12 for ChatGPT and 57% for Gemini on recent cohorts. Only 9% of consumers pay for more than one AI subscription, indicating "winner take most" dynamics.

Notable Product Launches

OpenAI's Sora standalone app achieved more than 12 million global downloads as a creative tool, though retention is sub-8% at day 30 (below the 30% benchmark for top consumer apps). Google's NotebookLM continues to be a bright spot, with web users more than doubling YoY and 8 million mobile MAUs. Grok grew from zero to 9.5M DAU and 38M MAU by mid-December.

5. Sector-Specific AI Developments

Healthcare AI: The Breakthrough Vertical

Healthcare alone captures nearly half of all vertical AI spend—approximately $1.5 billion, more than tripling from $450 million the year prior. The ambient scribe market reached $600 million in 2025 (+2.4x YoY), minting two new unicorns (Abridge and Ambience) alongside market leader Nuance's DAX Copilot. Clinicians spend roughly one hour documenting for every five hours of care; scribes that reduce documentation time by 50%+ dramatically reduce administrative burden.

Coding: The First "Killer Use Case"

Departmental AI spending hit $7.3 billion in 2025, with coding representing $4.0 billion (55% of departmental spend). This makes coding the largest category across the entire application layer. Fifty percent of developers now use AI coding tools daily (65% in top-quartile organizations). Teams report 15%+ velocity gains as AI tools now span the entire software development lifecycle.

Legal, Creator Tools, and Government

Beyond healthcare and coding, AI is gaining traction across underserved verticals. Legal has grown into a $650 million market led by companies like Eve. Creator tools reached $360 million. Government AI spending reached $350 million. These sectors historically defined by manual, unstructured workflows are now prime candidates for AI automation.

Vertical AI Total: $3.5 billion invested across vertical sectors in 2025, nearly 3x last year's spend. This represents a fundamental shift as AI-native software emerges to serve every sector of the economy, particularly those historically underserved by traditional software.

6. AI Infrastructure & Technology Stack

Infrastructure Investment Scale

The infrastructure layer captured $18 billion in 2025—up 2.0x from $9.2 billion in 2024. Hyperscalers have committed an estimated $300 billion-plus to capex in 2025 and increased that commitment for 2026. This massive investment reflects the voracious compute appetite of foundation model training and inference.

Incumbent Advantage in Infrastructure

Unlike the application layer where startups dominate, incumbents hold 56% of the infrastructure market. Companies like Databricks, Snowflake, MongoDB, and Datadog have enjoyed significant re-acceleration as even AI-native app builders continue choosing existing platforms for data management, workflow orchestration, and operations monitoring.

Inference Platform Innovation

Startup activity clusters around inference and compute, where AI-native vendors compete directly with hyperscaler developer platforms. Inference platforms like Fireworks, Baseten, Modal, and Together win on performance and developer experience—offering serverless, high-throughput, open-weight endpoints with 2x+ speedups through optimized serving stacks and tightly managed GPU fleets.

Open-Source Model Adoption

Despite Llama's stagnation (no major releases since April's Llama 4), it remains the most widely adopted open-weight model in the enterprise. However, overall enterprise open-source share declined from 19% last year to 11% today. Chinese models like Qwen, DeepSeek, Moonshot/Kimi, MiniMax, and Z AI's GLM are gaining visible traction among developers, though enterprises remain cautious.

7. Regulatory & Policy Developments

Federal Executive Order on AI

On December 11, 2025, President Trump issued "Ensuring a National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence," seeking to position the U.S. as the global leader in AI innovation. The order establishes that "United States AI companies must be free to innovate without cumbersome regulation," identifying state-level regulation as a barrier to national economic and security interests.

Key Directives

The executive order includes several significant provisions: (1) Creation of an AI Litigation Task Force within 30 days to oversee legal challenges to state AI laws; (2) Evaluation of state laws that "obstruct, hinder, or otherwise contravene" national AI policy within 90 days; (3) Restrictions on federal funding to states with obstructive AI laws; (4) Development of new federal AI reporting and disclosure standards within 90 days; (5) Preemption of state laws mandating "deceptive conduct" in AI models.

State Response & Legal Challenges

State lawmakers from both parties say they plan to keep passing AI laws despite the federal order. The attempt to preempt state law through conditions on congressionally authorized grants is likely to prompt independent legal challenges from state attorneys general and advocacy groups. California launched a 30-member AI council to guide state policy as a "counterweight" to federal efforts.

Regulatory Uncertainty: The order adds complexity to an already evolving AI regulatory landscape. While signaling a shift toward unified federal standards, the prospect of litigation and absence of comprehensive federal legislation will increase short-term uncertainty. Companies should anticipate prolonged parallel evolution of federal policy, state enforcement, and judicial interpretation.

8. Productivity & Economic Impact

Productivity Gains Materializing

Early evidence suggests AI is delivering real productivity improvements. The St. Louis Federal Reserve found that users of generative AI tools reported saving an average of 2.2 hours per 40-hour workweek. A Microsoft study identified the top 20 jobs at greatest risk of automation, including telephone operators, translators, ticket agents, telemarketers, brokerage clerks, and customer service reps.

Job Impact Realities

An MIT report estimated that 11.7% of the labor market could be automated by AI—representing about a 1.2 percentage point increase in annual productivity if occurred over a decade. The same report estimated around 100,000 jobs were lost due to AI in 2025, representing just 0.5% of all layoffs. This suggests measured rather than revolutionary employment impact.

Skill-Based Productivity Differences

AI productivity effects vary significantly by worker skill level. One telemarketing study found AI boosted productivity for skilled workers but reduced it for less-skilled workers. Another study found the opposite—less-skilled workers' productivity increased 30% versus 15% for more-skilled workers. A consulting study found AI increased errors by 19%, highlighting that results can be mixed.

Long-Term Economic Potential

The U.S. has experienced a productivity slowdown since 2007, with growth slumping to 1.6% annually compared to 2.3% from 1947-2007. AI offers potential to restore this productivity growth, not through dystopian transformation but through steady, incremental improvements across the economy. As a general-purpose technology, AI could bring us closer to the 2.3% benchmark if it reaches sufficient scale and capability.

9. Emerging Trends & 2026 Predictions

Agentic AI Reality Check

Despite the hype around "AI agents," real production architectures remain surprisingly simple. Only 16% of enterprise and 27% of startup deployments qualify as true agents—systems where LLMs plan and execute actions, observe feedback, and adapt behavior. Most implementations use fixed-sequence or routing-based workflows around single model calls. Prompt design remains the dominant technique, followed by RAG.

Platform Wars: ChatGPT's App Ecosystem

OpenAI is launching "Apps," infrastructure for third-party developers to build experiences within ChatGPT. This represents a potential to become the first true new consumer platform in more than a decade. However, the company will fight against providing enough space to power magical consumer experiences within the constraints of the existing ChatGPT interface.

Google's Search Innovation

Google demoed "Disco," which will remix search tabs into interactive, custom web apps—described as perhaps the "biggest swing" Google has ever taken in innovating on its core UI. This comes as AI Mode debuted in Google Search in May, though only about 2% of weekly users are currently interacting with it.

Physical AI & Robotics

Progress in automating physical jobs has been slower than knowledge work automation, but AI-powered robots are advancing. "Physical AI" integrating models with robotics will be key to boosting productivity in factories, farming, and restaurants. Humanoid robotics is emerging as a solution for not-well-defined real-world problems, though applications remain early-stage.

10. Notable Company Developments

Meta's Strategic Moves

Meta acquired startup Manus to advance its artificial intelligence capabilities. The company launched a standalone Meta AI app in April, which grew to 4.2M DAU and 26.6M MAU by mid-December, primarily outside the U.S. Meta poached an all-star research team and holds massive distribution advantage across Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp.

Anthropic's $10B Financing

Anthropic is reportedly lining up $10 billion in financing underwritten by a $350 billion valuation, with potential backing from GIC and Coatue. This reflects intensifying competition in the foundation model market and massive capital requirements for training and infrastructure.

NVIDIA's Potential AI21 Acquisition

NVIDIA is reportedly in advanced talks to acquire LLM startup AI21 Labs for $3 billion. This would represent NVIDIA's strategic move deeper into the AI software stack, complementing its hardware dominance. The deal highlights the convergence of infrastructure and model development.

Accenture-Anthropic Partnership

Accenture and Anthropic launched a multi-year partnership to drive enterprise AI innovation. The partnership will train approximately 30,000 professionals globally, helping enterprises implement Anthropic's AI models responsibly. This reflects the growing enterprise services market around AI deployment.

HEINEKEN's Power Platform Implementation

HEINEKEN uses Microsoft Power Platform to streamline operations, with over 7,500 makers building thousands of apps and automations. They're now developing AI-driven solutions to enhance productivity and digital transformation across the organization, showcasing enterprise-scale AI integration.
