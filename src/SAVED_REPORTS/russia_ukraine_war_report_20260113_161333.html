Russia-Ukraine War: Weekly Assessment

Comprehensive Analysis of Frontline Updates, Diplomatic Developments, and Strategic Shifts
Report Period: January 6-12, 2026 | Report Date: January 13, 2026

Executive Summary

  • Escalation Through Advanced Weapons: Russia deployed its Oreshnik intermediate-range ballistic missile (IRBM) for only the second time, striking western Ukraine and sending a clear warning to NATO allies amid diplomatic negotiations.
  • Diplomatic Momentum Fragile: Western allies made "major progress" on security guarantees for Ukraine at the Paris summit, with plans for European troop deployments and a US-led ceasefire monitoring mechanism, though Moscow has rejected key elements.
  • Frontline Pressure Mounts: Russian forces continue incremental advances around Pokrovsk, Kupyansk, and Kostiantynivka while Ukraine maintains drone fire control that constrains Russian resupply within 5-7 km of frontlines.
  • Humanitarian Crisis Deepens: 2025 was the deadliest year for civilians in Ukraine since 2022, with massive Russian attacks on critical infrastructure leaving nearly 6,000 apartment buildings in Kyiv without heat during winter temperatures.
  • Sanctions Regime Expands: Trump greenlights a bipartisan sanctions bill that would impose 500% tariffs on countries buying Russian oil, while EU continues importing Russian LNG despite pledges to phase it out by 2027.
