## 3. Technical Breakthroughs (Laser Ablation & Robotic Capture)

### Laser Ablation Technology:
- **Approach:** High-powered lasers vaporize surface material of debris, producing thrust to gradually change orbit
- **Status:** Publicly available sources describe techniques but do not specify concrete TRL levels
- **Research:** NASA developing "Rapid Response Debris Removal Using Reconfigurable Space-Based Laser Network"
- **Applications:** Ground-based and orbital laser systems under development

### Robotic Capture:
- **Current Implementations:** Multiple startups testing robotic arms, capture nets, and harpoon systems
- **TRL Status:** Not publicly specified in 2025 sources
- **Key Challenge:** Precise proximity operations and capture mechanisms for non-cooperative targets

### Academic Research:
- MDPI studies on laser beam shaping techniques for debris removal
- University of Sussex thesis on LEO debris mitigation using high-power pulsed lasers
- JAPCC analysis of optical tracking and laser-based removal

## 5. Geopolitical Implications (ASAT + Space Security)

### ASAT Test Controversies (2021-2025):
- **Key Actors:** United States, Russia, China, India, North Korea
- **Concern:** Rapid development of kinetic and nuclear-armed ASAT weapons
- **Debris Threat:** Long-lasting debris fields from kinetic tests

### Space Security & Arms Control:
- **Treaty at Risk:** Outer Space Treaty jeopardized by weapons development
- **Proposals:**
  - Bilateral US-China kinetic ASAT test ban
  - Multilateral norms for responsible behavior
  - New arms control frameworks

### Dual-Use Technology Concerns:
- Debris removal capabilities could be repurposed for ASAT attacks
- Robotic capture systems for debris could disable satellites
- Laser ablation for debris could damage operational spacecraft

### Country-Specific Positions:
- **United States:** Intelligence confirms Russia developing nuclear ASAT (2024)
- **China:** Expanding counterspace capabilities
- **Russia:** Nuclear ASAT development confirmed
- **India/North Korea:** Emerging ASAT capabilities
