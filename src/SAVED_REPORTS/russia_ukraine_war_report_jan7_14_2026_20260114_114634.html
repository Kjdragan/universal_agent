Russia-Ukraine War Weekly Report

Reporting Period: January 7-14, 2026 | Day 1,416-1,420 of the War

Executive Summary

The week of January 7-14, 2026, witnessed a dangerous escalation in the Russia-Ukraine war, marked by Russia's first combat use of the nuclear-capable Oreshnik hypersonic ballistic missile and a renewed intensive campaign against Ukraine's energy infrastructure. The conflict entered a concerning new phase as Russian forces launched systematic attacks on civilian heating and power systems during harsh winter conditions, leaving hundreds of thousands of Ukrainians without heat. Diplomatic efforts faced significant setbacks as Russian officials rejected key elements of Western peace proposals, with Vladimir Putin reiterating that Russia's war aims would be met "unconditionally." International support for Ukraine continued, with the European Commission advancing new financial support packages and NATO allies reaffirming their commitment to Ukrainian defense.
