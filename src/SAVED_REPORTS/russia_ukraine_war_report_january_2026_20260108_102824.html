Comprehensive Report on Days 1,409-1,414

Executive Summary

As the war enters its fourth calendar year, Russian forces have made significant territorial gains in 2025, capturing more Ukrainian territory than in the previous two years combined. The current situation is marked by intensified Russian attacks on Ukraine's energy infrastructure, ongoing peace negotiations with uncertain outcomes, and growing Western involvement in security guarantees.

  • Russian Advances: Russian forces captured more Ukrainian territory in 2025 than in 2023 and 2024 combined
  • Energy Infrastructure: Renewed Russian drone and missile campaigns targeting Ukraine's power grid and southeastern regions
  • Security Guarantees: US and European allies finalizing binding security commitments for Ukraine
  • Western Troop Deployment: UK and France pledge multinational force; Russia declares any Western troops "legitimate targets"
  • Morale on Front: Ukrainian troops express war fatigue and skepticism about peace prospects for 2026

According to battlefield analysis, the Russian army captured more Ukrainian territory in 2025 than in the previous two years combined. This shift in momentum has fundamentally altered the calculus of the conflict and strengthened Russia's position in ongoing peace negotiations.

Key Frontline Developments

Donetsk Region: Russian forces have made significant advances in the Donetsk region, with intense fighting around Pokrovsk and surrounding areas. Putin has long coveted this region, and it remains the nexus of the fighting.

Ukrainian military intelligence chief General Kyrylo Budanov warned that Russia's aim for 2026 is to seize all of the Donetsk and Zaporizhzhia regions and drive farther into the Dnipro region. Russian forces exploited Ukrainian defensive concentrations around Pokrovsk to advance more quickly in the Zaporizhzhia region and eastern parts of Dnipro region.

"They said they only needed the Donetsk region, yet look how many kilometers they've taken in the Dnipro region already." — Ukrainian soldier with call sign 'Dyak', 68th Jager Brigade

Drone Warfare Dominance

The battlefield has been transformed by drone warfare, making troop movements far more dangerous for both sides. Russian forces have adapted their tactics, switching from large-scale assaults to using smaller, more mobile groups of soldiers. The New Year's period saw intense drone activity from both sides, with reconnaissance flights and strikes continuing through the holiday.

2. Energy Infrastructure Under Attack

Blackouts in Southeastern Ukraine

On January 7-8, 2026, Russian drone attacks plunged two southeastern regions of Ukraine into near-total blackout. President Volodymyr Zelenskiy stated these strikes were aimed at "breaking" Ukraine's resolve as winter temperatures plunge as low as -20 degrees Celsius (-4 degrees Fahrenheit).

Civilian Impact: Authorities in Kyiv warned residents to stock up on water, batteries, and warm clothing in anticipation of further attacks on energy infrastructure during the coldest period of winter.

Pattern of Attacks

Russia has intensified its targeting of Ukraine's energy infrastructure in recent weeks, a pattern that mirrors previous winter campaigns. The attacks aim to:

  • Disrupt civilian morale and daily life
  • Pressure Ukraine's industrial capacity in the southeast
  • Force diversion of military resources to protect critical infrastructure

In addition to energy targets, Russia attacked two seaports in Ukraine's Odesa region on January 7, killing one person and injuring eight others, according to Ukrainian officials.

3. Western Involvement and Security Guarantees

UK-France Multinational Force Pledge

On January 6, 2026, UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer and French President Emmanuel Macron signed a "declaration of intent" to deploy a multinational force to guarantee Ukraine's security in the event of a peace deal. Starmer stated that any deployment of UK forces would be subject to a parliamentary vote.

Russia's Response

Russian Foreign Ministry spokesperson Maria Zakharova issued a stern warning on January 8:

"The Russian Foreign Ministry warns that the deployment of military units, military facilities, warehouses, and other infrastructure of Western countries on the territory of Ukraine will be classified as foreign intervention... These warnings have been voiced repeatedly at the highest level and remain relevant." — Maria Zakharova, Russian Foreign Ministry Spokesperson

Escalation Risk: Russia has long opposed any Western troop presence in Ukraine. The deployment of Western forces could significantly escalate the conflict and draw NATO countries more directly into the war.

US Security Guarantees

President Zelenskiy announced on January 7 that the text of a bilateral security guarantee between Kyiv and Washington was "essentially ready" to be finalized with US President Donald Trump. This marks a significant development in US-Ukraine relations, which have seen considerable volatility over the past year.

The United States for the first time on January 6 backed a broad coalition of Ukraine's allies in vowing to provide security guarantees that leaders said would include binding commitments to support the country if it is attacked by Russia again.

4. Peace Negotiations and Diplomatic Developments

Territory: The Core Obstacle

President Zelenskiy stated on January 6 that US and Ukrainian officials discussed "some ideas" to address the issue of territory during peace talks, describing the matter as "the biggest remaining obstacle in the process."

The territorial dispute centers on:

  • Russia's occupation of Crimea since 2014
  • Russian-controlled areas in Donetsk, Luhansk, Zaporizhzhia, and Kherson regions
  • Ukraine's insistence on territorial integrity and 1991 borders

Evolving US Position

The US-Ukraine relationship has experienced dramatic shifts over the past year:

  • Early 2025: Then-President-elect Trump boasted he could end the war in 24 hours
  • February 2025: President Trump berated President Zelensky in the Oval Office, threatening to cut off aid
  • Late 2025: Relationship improved, with Trump supporting security guarantees
  • January 2026: US backing binding security commitments for Ukraine

President Trump initially set deadlines of Thanksgiving, then Christmas for Ukraine to agree to a peace proposal. Three days after Christmas, Trump stood alongside Zelensky and eschewed any timeline, indicating a more flexible approach.

Reconstruction Planning

Even as the war continues, planning for Ukraine's reconstruction is underway. The US International Development Finance Corporation (DFC) launched an online portal on January 7 for applicants to submit projects to a new US-Ukraine reconstruction fund, with first investments expected in coming months. Analysts suggest that if President Trump is successful in pushing through a truce, a massive reconstruction boom could begin almost immediately.

5. Morale and Perspectives from the Front

Ukrainian Troop Sentiment

Reporting from the front lines on New Year's Day revealed widespread skepticism among Ukrainian troops about prospects for peace in 2026. Soldiers expressed exhaustion and uncertainty about the future:

"It's hard to make any plans." — Ukrainian officer with call sign 'Sam', Da Vinci Wolves battalion
"For all Russia's words, Putin is not showing that he's ready for peace." — UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer

Personal Impact

Major Serhii Filimonov, 31, commander of the 108th "Da Vinci Wolves" battalion, reflected on the personal cost of the war:

"I only hope to finish the war before my son grows up, so that he doesn't have to go fight." — Major Serhii Filimonov, who has two children, ages 8 and 2

Many soldiers interviewed said they did not believe the war would end in 2026, with some estimating it could continue for at least two more years. Despite the fatigue, Major Filimonov noted that Russia had failed to achieve its key objectives for 2025, such as capturing Pokrovsk or other key cities, which gave Ukrainian forces faith that "everything will be OK."

Rejection of Territorial Concessions

Among front-line troops, there was strong opposition to any territorial concessions to Russia:

"We want a full victory — to take back all of Donbas." — Ukrainian drone pilot with call sign 'Shanghai'

Strategic Reality: While Ukrainian troops reject territorial concessions, the battlefield reality of Russian gains in 2025 has created a significant disconnect between frontline sentiment and the emerging parameters of potential peace agreements.

6. Russian Domestic Developments and International Support

Attacks on Russian Territory

Ukraine continues to strike targets within Russia, including an oil depot in the Belgorod region. Firefighters responded to a blaze following a Ukrainian drone attack on January 7, according to Russian state TV. In the Black Sea, a Russia-bound oil tanker suffered a drone attack that prompted it to request Turkish coastguard assistance and divert from its course.

International Pressure on Russia

Senator reports indicate President Trump will allow a Russia sanctions bill to advance in Congress. The legislation would impose sanctions on countries doing business with Russia. Meanwhile, German Foreign Minister Johann Wadephul expressed confidence that NATO would remain united, with the defense alliance continuing its current structure despite Russian pressure.

Energy Sector Developments

In a sign of ongoing Western economic pressure, private equity group Quantum Energy Partners is working on a bid that could involve US oil major Chevron for the international assets of Russian oil major Lukoil, according to sources familiar with the matter.

Strategic Implications

Russia's Position: Russia's battlefield gains in 2025 have strengthened Putin's negotiating position. The territorial advances, particularly in Donetsk and Zaporizhzhia, have given Russia leverage to demand Ukrainian concessions. Russia's escalation of energy infrastructure attacks demonstrates a continued willingness to inflict civilian suffering as leverage.

Ukraine's Position: Ukraine faces significant challenges, including manpower shortages, territorial losses, and war fatigue among troops and civilians. However, the prospect of binding Western security guarantees represents a diplomatic achievement that could provide long-term deterrence against renewed Russian aggression.

Western Unity: The commitment of UK and French troops to a potential security force, alongside US security guarantees, signals sustained Western commitment to Ukraine's security. However, Russia's sharp warning against Western troop deployment raises the risk of direct confrontation.

Looking Ahead to 2026

The war's trajectory in 2026 will depend on several critical factors:

  • Whether Russia can capitalize on its 2025 gains to seize more territory in Donetsk, Zaporizhzhia, and Dnipro regions
  • Final terms of US and European security guarantees for Ukraine
  • Ukraine's ability to mobilize additional troops and resources
  • Impact of continued Western military aid and economic sanctions on Russia
  • Success or failure of peace negotiations to bridge the territorial dispute

Bottom Line: Despite intense diplomatic activity and shifting battlefield dynamics, there is no clear path to a rapid end to the conflict. Both sides continue to prepare for prolonged fighting, even as negotiations continue. The war appears likely to extend well into 2026, with significant implications for European security and the global order.
