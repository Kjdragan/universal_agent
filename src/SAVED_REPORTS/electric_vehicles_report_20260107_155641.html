Electric Vehicle Developments Report

Research Period: July 2025 - January 2026

Report Generated: January 7, 2026

Executive Summary

The electric vehicle industry experienced a transformative period from July 2025 to January 2026, characterized by groundbreaking battery technology advances, solid-state battery production readiness, and significant charging infrastructure expansion. The U.S. market projected 2.25 million EV sales in 2025, while globally EVs reached approximately 25% of new car sales. This report examines key technological breakthroughs, market dynamics, policy shifts, and infrastructure developments reshaping the EV landscape.

Technology Advances

BREAKTHROUGH: Production-Ready Solid-State Batteries

As of January 6, 2026, solid-state batteries have moved from research to production reality:

Donut Lab's Revolutionary Battery:
  • Energy Density: 400 Wh/kg packs—significantly higher than conventional lithium-ion
  • Charging Speed: Can fully charge in just five minutes
  • Lifetime Performance: First US-market EV launching with 370-mile range using lifetime solid-state battery
  • Production Status: Already in production vehicles, not just prototypes

Source: Donut Lab CES Announcement; Top Gear, January 6, 2026

Industry Validation

Donut Lab CEO Marko Lehtimäki emphasized the company waited to announce until the technology was "fully tested, validated, and already operating in vehicles," distinguishing this from typical pre-production announcements. First customer deliveries are targeted for Q1 2026.

Source: Donut Lab Blog, January 2026

Solid-State Battery Ecosystem

The solid-state battery landscape is expanding rapidly:

  • Ducati V21L: Prototype race bike featuring all-solid-state battery unveiled at IAA Mobility 2025
  • Mercedes EQS: Testing solid-state batteries in luxury vehicles
  • Verge Motorcycles: First customer deliveries targeted for Q1 2026

Source: InsideEVs, BatteryTechOnline, 2025

Silver-Based Solid-State Batteries

Emerging research suggests silver-based solid-state batteries could represent the future of both EVs and energy storage, offering potential advantages in performance and sustainability.

Source: CarbonCredits.com, January 5, 2026

Market & Industry Developments

U.S. EV Sales: 2.25 Million Projected for 2025

As 2025 drew to a close, the U.S. EV market remained on a strong upward trend with projected sales of 2.25 million units by year's end. Despite October's dip following federal tax credit expiration, Q3 2025 sales showed 40% year-over-year growth.

Source: Morgan Lewis, December 15, 2025

Global EV Penetration

Global Reach: EVs made up approximately 25% of global car sales in 2025

Growth in emerging markets has turbocharged global EV sales, with over a quarter of new cars sold being electric worldwide. However, the U.S. lags behind at only 10% EV adoption, indicating significant room for growth.

Source: Ember Energy, December 16, 2025; Yale Climate Connections, December 9, 2025

November 2025 Market Dynamics

Year-to-date EV sales remained 2.1% above 2024 levels, though November showed weakness with EV share of total sales falling to 5.4%—the lowest since April 2025.

Source: Cox Automotive EV Market Monitor, November 2025

2026 Projections

S&P Global Mobility projects U.S. auto sales volumes to reach 15.89 million units in 2026, a decline of 2.5% from 2025's projected 16.3 million units, reflecting an uncertain environment for auto sales.

Source: S&P Global Mobility, July 2025

Market Competition: Tesla vs. BYD

Chinese automaker BYD has taken the lead in EV sales, reshaping charging infrastructure and challenging Tesla's dominance. This shift reflects broader changes in the global competitive landscape.

Source: EV Infrastructure News, January 5, 2026

Infrastructure Developments

Tesla Supercharger Network Opens to Non-Tesla EVs

Tesla's NACS-based Supercharger network has expanded access significantly: by the end of 2025, roughly 67.5% of Tesla Supercharger stations were open to non-Tesla EVs, dramatically improving charging options for all EV drivers.

Source: AInvest, December 3, 2025

Charging Infrastructure Boom

The EV charging infrastructure sector is experiencing strategic expansion focusing on:

  • Standardization: Industry-wide adoption of common charging standards (NACS gaining dominance)
  • Scalability: Rapid deployment of new charging stations to meet growing demand
  • Accessibility: Opening proprietary networks to all EV brands

Source: AInvest, December 3, 2025

2025 EV Trends

Key infrastructure trends shaping the industry:

  • Lower electric car prices improving affordability
  • Expansion of charging stations increasing convenience
  • Greater charging plug compatibility reducing friction

Source: GreenLancer, 2025

Policy & Regulation

Federal Tax Credit Impact

The expiration of federal EV tax credits in October 2025 caused a temporary sales dip, highlighting the importance of government incentives in driving adoption. Quarterly growth, however, remained robust with Q3 showing 40% year-over-year increases.

Source: Morgan Lewis, December 15, 2025

State-Level Coordination

Washington State's Interagency Electric Vehicle Coordinating Council (EVCC), established in 2022, continues to enable state-level coordination on EV infrastructure and policy development, serving as a model for other states.

Source: Washington State Department of Commerce, 2025

EU Policy Backtracking

The European Union announced plans to ditch a controversial ban on combustion engine cars starting in 2035 after months of debate, reflecting political tensions in the transition to electric mobility.

Source: Bloomberg, December 16, 2025

U.S. Automaker Pullback

U.S. automakers have begun shifting production from electric vehicles back to gas-powered vehicles, accompanied by:

  • Reduced EV spending
  • Workforce layoffs
  • Delayed EV model launches

This pullback reflects market uncertainties and political headwinds facing EV adoption in the U.S.

Source: Institute for Energy Research, December 18, 2025

Consumer & Cultural Developments

Political Polarization

Electric vehicles became increasingly politicized in 2025, with commentators tracing the roots of this debate back 25 years to the Toyota Prius. The "poisonous debate" around EVs reflects broader cultural and political divisions.

Source: New York Times, December 27, 2025

Enhanced Driving Experience

New EVs feature immersive technology making driving seamless and safe:

  • Augmented reality integration
  • Advanced driver assistance systems
  • Seamless connectivity and entertainment features

Source: ABC7 Los Angeles, December 16, 2025

Aftermarket Tensions

EV powertrains are causing tension in the automotive aftermarket due to:

  • Different maintenance requirements vs. internal combustion engines
  • Compact motor and battery packaging affecting service procedures
  • Traditional mechanics needing new training for EV systems

Source: Ars Technica, December 17, 2025

  • 2027 Chevy Bolt - Return of affordable EV

Technology Trajectory

  • Solid-State Batteries: Production ramp-up throughout 2026-2027
  • Charging Infrastructure: Continued expansion and standardization
  • Price Parity: Approaching cost parity with internal combustion vehicles
  • Emerging Markets: Driving global growth as U.S. and EU markets mature

Market Projections

Despite near-term headwinds including political polarization and automaker pullbacks, long-term projections remain bullish due to:

  • Technology advantages (batteries, performance, lower operating costs)
  • Improving charging infrastructure
  • Corporate climate commitments and regulations
  • Growing global adoption outside U.S.
