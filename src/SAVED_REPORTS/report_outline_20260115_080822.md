# Russia-Ukraine War Report Outline (January 8-15, 2026)

## Executive Summary
- Conflict approaching 4-year mark
- Transition from maneuver warfare to attrition
- High casualties on both sides with minimal territorial gains
- Escalation in aerial warfare and infrastructure targeting
- Stalled peace negotiations despite diplomatic efforts

## Military Updates & Battlefield Dynamics
- Russian casualties: 20,000-35,000 troops killed monthly
- Ukrainian recruitment struggles and drone warfare expansion
- Territorial changes: minimal gains for massive losses
- Drone warfare evolution (fiber-optic drones, FPV units)
- Long-range attack statistics (54,000 drones, 1,900 missiles in 2025)
- Specific battlefield advances and operational stalls

## Strategic Escalation & Critical Infrastructure
- Oreshnik hypersonic missile deployment
- Targeting of energy grids during freezing temperatures
- Zaporizhzhia Nuclear Power Plant militarization
- Winter attacks on civilian infrastructure
- Black Sea drone strikes on oil tankers

## Nuclear Concerns
- Military vehicles at Zaporizhzhia NPP (violates Geneva Conventions)
- Nuclear-capable Oreshnik missile near Poland border
- IAEA access restrictions and ongoing risks

## Diplomatic Efforts & Peace Negotiations
- Stalled US-led peace talks
- Territorial disputes: four regions, Crimea, NATO membership
- "Coalition of the Willing" security guarantees
- NATO troop deployment debates and Russian warnings
- EU's €90 billion loan package for 2026-2027
- International sanctions and economic warfare

## Humanitarian Situation
- 10.8 million people in need of assistance
- 3.7 million internally displaced persons
- 2025 deadliest year for civilians: 2,514 killed, 12,142 injured
- Winter crisis: -12°C to -19°C temperatures
- Energy infrastructure damage causing heating/electricity blackouts
- Mental health crisis affecting children and population
- Aid worker safety: 8 killed, 47 injured in 2025

## Economic Warfare & International Support
- Russian LNG exports to EU: €7.2bn in 2025
- NATO support: €50 billion in 2024, €35 billion in 2025
- US, UK, EU funding packages and military assistance
- Drone production partnerships (UK Octopus interceptors)
- Impact of sanctions on Russian military production

## Conclusion & Outlook
- 2026 predictions: war likely to continue
- Scenarios: frozen conflict vs. continued fighting
