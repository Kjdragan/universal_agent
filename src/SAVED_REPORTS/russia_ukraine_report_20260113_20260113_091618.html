I. Military Developments

New Weapons Systems

Geran-5 Jet-Powered Drone

On January 11, Ukraine's military intelligence (HUR) reported the first combat deployment of Russia's new Geran-5 jet-powered attack drone. Key characteristics:

  • Dimensions: 6 meters long, 5.5-meter wingspan
  • Warhead: 90 kilograms (200 pounds)
  • Range: Nearly 1,000 kilometers (620 miles)
  • Engine: Telefly jet engine with greater thrust than Geran-3 model
  • Navigation: 12-channel Cometa satellite system, Raspberry Pi-based tracker with 3G/4G

Source: Kyiv Independent, Jan 11, 2026

Oreshnik Hypersonic Missile

Russia continued employment of the Oreshnik intermediate-range ballistic missile system:

  • Confirmed strike on Ukrainian defense industrial enterprise in Lviv City
  • Missile speeds recorded at approximately 13,000 km/h (over 8,000 mph)
  • Designed to carry nuclear weapons; conventional use demonstrates strategic messaging
  • ISW assessment indicates strikes aimed at deterring Western military support, particularly potential troop deployments

Source: ISW Assessments Jan 9-10, 2026; Al Jazeera Day 1,417

Frontline Situation

According to Ukrainian military reports:

  • January 11: 139 combat clashes; Russia launched 33 air strikes, deployed over 4,430 drones, carried out 2,830 attacks
  • Territorial changes: Russian forces claimed control of village of Bilohirya in Zaporizhia region
  • Advances: Russian forces advanced near villages of Markove and Kleban-Byk in Donetsk region

Source: Al Jazeera Day 1,417

VII. Analysis and Outlook

Strategic Assessment

The week's developments indicate several important trends:

  1. Escalation Dynamics: Russia's systematic targeting of energy infrastructure represents a strategy to break Ukrainian civilian morale and military capacity during the winter months.
  2. Technological Adaptation: The deployment of Geran-5 drones demonstrates Russia's continued adaptation and modernization of unmanned systems, responding to Ukraine's successful drone warfare tactics.
  3. Strategic Messaging: Oreshnik missile strikes near NATO borders appear calibrated to intimidate Western allies and deter discussions of troop deployments to Ukraine.
  4. Ukrainian Resilience: Despite infrastructure damage, Ukraine maintains capability for deep strikes into Russian territory, targeting energy and military assets.
  5. Diplomatic Complexity: Paris talks suggest progress toward security guarantees, but Russian objections to Western troop presence and US-Russia negotiations create uncertainty.

Key Questions for Coming Weeks

  • Will Western security guarantees be finalized before potential peace negotiations?
  • Can Ukraine's energy grid withstand continued Russian attacks?
  • How will Russia respond to Ukrainian strikes on Caspian Sea oil infrastructure?
  • Will US-Russia direct negotiations produce results, and what are the red lines for Ukraine and European allies?
