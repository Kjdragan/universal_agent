Executive Summary

The period from July 2025 to January 2026 witnessed unprecedented acceleration in artificial intelligence capabilities, adoption, and regulation. OpenAI's GPT-5.2 set new benchmarks for long-context reasoning and tool use, while generative AI attracted $33.9 billion in global private investment. ChatGPT reached penetration of 10% of the world's adult population, and the EU AI Act's operational rules took effect, establishing the first comprehensive regulatory framework for AI systems.

Policy & Regulation

EU AI Act Operationalization (August 2025)

The European Union's AI Act established the world's first comprehensive AI regulatory framework. Key developments:

  • Code of Practice: July 2025 release of Code of Practice for General-Purpose AI, imposing mandatory testing, risk mitigation, and transparency duties on high-risk systems
  • Safety Standards: European Commission tasked CEN-CENELEC with drafting accompanying safety standards
  • Implementation Timeline: Draft guidelines published in July 2025 with rules taking effect August 2025

Source: CSET Georgetown, July 30, 2025; EU Digital Strategy, August 2025

US Regulatory Landscape

  • Federal-Level Stalled: Congressional efforts to regulate AI at the federal level fell short in 2025
  • Executive Actions: Trump signed executive order seeking to block states from regulating AI companies, creating tension between federal and state approaches
  • State-Level Action: Individual states like California moved forward with AI regulations requiring disaster plan disclosures from AI makers

Source: NBC News, December 11, 2025; CalMatters, December 31, 2025
