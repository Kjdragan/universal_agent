## Idea

The deliverable for the landing-page **discovery audience** (Audience B — small/mid business owners who haven't used an AI agent and don't yet know what's possible). Audience A's product-as-evidence hero (IDEA_01) doesn't serve this audience well: a buyer with no frame to decode a screen recording sees "someone using QuickBooks," and the lo-fi evidentiary aesthetic lands as inscrutable rather than persuasive. The reveal-video is what turns *"I didn't know agents could do this"* into *"I should see this."*

Per FT-R4 (revising FT-R3), this is **structurally distinct** from IDEA_01 — not an extension of it:

| | Evidence-only (IDEA_01) | Time-travel / Revelation-via-specificity (IDEA_02) |
|---|---|---|
| Question answered | Does this actually work? | What does my life look like if I have this? |
| Tense | Past-tense | Future-tense |
| What's on screen | Real product, real data | Real product + the operator's life around it (closed laptop, kid pickup, dinner) |
| Conversion lever | Trust | Aspiration |
| Audience disposition | Already wants the thing | Hasn't yet decided they want the thing |

The two briefs are **one capture, two productions, shared raw footage** at the production-pipeline level. They are NOT "two cuts of one asset." The footage layer is shared — the operator films once. Post-production is two distinct workstreams: the recognition cut leans on the screen-as-is; the discovery cut adds annotations, named-notification labels, time-jump composition, voiceover beats, and ambient-texture composition. **That's real production work, not editing.** Treat IDEA_02 as a separate production that re-uses IDEA_01's footage layer.

### The construct: time-travel diptych = revelation-via-specificity

> **Crossing-convergence callout.** FT-R3 (round 10, 17:50) and Grounder-R3 (round 11, 17:55) were dual-sent **within five minutes of each other, without either having read the other's R3.** FT named the construct **TT — time-travel diptych** with the scenario *"A Tuesday in 2026 vs. a Tuesday in 2027. Same owner, same Tuesday, same coffee, same office."* Grounder named it **revelation-via-specificity** with the example *"You know that Tuesday-night QuickBooks ritual? Here's an agent doing it while you're at your kid's soccer game."* Same construct, two vocabularies. One ultra-specific scenario, named precisely, with the operator's life visible around the agent's work, producing aspiration through observable cause-and-effect rather than explanation. When two dialogue agents reach the same answer from opposite starting points (FT from style, Grounder from cognitive task) **without seeing each other's work**, the answer is durable. This is the strongest robustness signal of the entire session.

For naming inside this brief and downstream: use **time-travel diptych (TT)** when describing the production construct (it carries the shot-language explicitly), use **revelation-via-specificity** when describing the cognitive principle the construct implements.

### Three cuts of one capture (per FT-R4, revising R3's "two cuts")

1. **Pain cut** — 2026 frame alone, no resolution, ~6-second hook intended as the audience-A-overflow entry into the dual frame. The viewer sees their own pain on screen and clicks through.
2. **Discovery cut** — 2027 frame alone, ambient agent work as a *curiosity hook*. The viewer asks: *"Wait, what's happening on his screen while he's eating?"* Ambient texture in the right frame is real screen content composited into live-action — notifications sliding in, invoices getting paid, reviews getting answered. Per FT-R4 ratifying FT-3's absorbed convention: notifications can be signed ("Maya replied to a Yelp review." / "Carlos drafted the invoice.") — *keep frame, drop format*.
3. **Dual frame** — full diptych, side-by-side. For engaged viewers; post-click or scroll-revealed. This is where aspiration crystallises. **Headline copy is path-conditional:** Path A may use *"This happened in 12 months"* (longitudinal documentary claim — earned by the longitudinal capture). Path A-bridge must use *"What your Tuesday could look like, this time next year"* (colloquial future-projection — does not assert documentary fact). The interval phrasing distinguishes the two productions on the page; honesty floor is non-negotiable.

Routing the three cuts is the job of IDEA_03's UX-C invisible-segmentation v1.5 destination. Audience signals (channel-mix at v1 / IP+UTM at v1.5) decide which cut the visitor lands on; the dual frame is the convergence destination after either single-frame click-through.

### Two paths (production-pipeline branching)

The construct above is *what* gets produced. The path branching is *how* — driven entirely by whether the operator can credibly do longitudinal capture.

- **Path A-bridge — TT as composited approximation. THE V1 RECOMMENDED SHIP.** Same diptych structure, but the 2027 frame uses composited screen content (real product, real Slack, real invoices) over a live-action shot taken the same day as the 2026 frame. The "same person a year apart" is presented as future-state visualisation, not documentary. v1-shippable today with the operator's available customer and product. **Honesty floor:** copy must read *"What your Tuesday could look like, this time next year"* (or similar future-projection language) — never *"twelve months later"* or *"this happened in 12 months."* Reserve documentary language for Path A only. The 2026 capture for Path A-bridge IS the 2026 capture for an eventual Path A graduation — see Path A below.
- **Path A — Longitudinal-capture TT. THE GRADUATION TARGET, not an alternative anchor.** The version Path A-bridge becomes after 12 months of customer deployment. FT-R4's defense: *"It's a longitudinal capture, not a staged before/after. Shoot the 2026 frame today with a real customer; shoot the 2027 frame after the agent is deployed for them. Both moments are real. The only 'fictional' element is 'this is the same person a year apart' — a framing device, not a fabrication."* **Path A is not a first-class alternative to Path A-bridge; it is what Path A-bridge graduates to.** Operator ships Path A-bridge as v1, captures the 2026 frame today with real customer consent (which doubles as Path A's eventual 2026 frame), then 12 months later re-shoots the same customer's 2027 frame for real, swaps it into the diptych, and earns the right to use longitudinal documentary copy. The graduation moment is a credibility upgrade, not a re-production. Requires (a) a real customer running the agent today, (b) 12 months of agent deployment for that customer, (c) consent for both shoots up front. Q3 2027 graduation timeline for an operator shipping Path A-bridge today.
- **Path B — Below-fold narrated walkthrough of IDEA_01's hero footage. THE RECOGNITION-DEEPENING — NOT a discovery-video.** Grounder's earlier counter-proposal from Thread D. Longer (60–90s) operator-voiceover walkthrough of the same product session as IDEA_01's hero. **Path B is the right answer when Audience B is a minority of traffic. It is NOT a discovery-video; it is a recognition-deepening. If the channel-mix diagnostic points to Audience B as dominant, Path B should not be selected — defer the second video entirely until Path A-bridge is feasible.** Shipping Path B to a discovery-dominant audience is the wrong job: it answers Audience A's question (does this work?) more thoroughly while not answering Audience B's question (what does my life look like?) at all. Path B is a real option only when the diagnostic confirms recognition dominance.

### Two-tier diagnostic for path selection

Both tiers should be applied. Tier 2 is the fallback for solo / pre-launch operators where tier 1 isn't available.

**Tier 1 — Analytics diagnostic (when traffic data exists).**
Run all three checks; pick the path that two of three signals favour.

- Acquisition channel breakdown over the last 90 days, paid leads only.
- Self-described AI-familiarity at signup (free-text or single-question).
- Search-query analysis: broad AI / automation terms vs operator-pain terms.
- Decision rule: if Audience B is ≥ ~25% of qualified traffic, Path A (or Path A-bridge) is justified. Below ~15%, Path B. Middle band is operator judgment.

**Tier 2 — Channel-mix fallback heuristic** (codified per Arbiter's locked positions, applied where Tier 1 isn't available).

| Channel category | Examples | Implies audience | Recommended path |
|---|---|---|---|
| Recognition-skewed | Partner referrals; warm intros; founder-network outreach; niche-community DMs; "we built this for you" cold-list segmented on current pain | Audience A dominant | Path B only (deepening) |
| Discovery-skewed | Content marketing on broad AI / automation terms; paid search on "what can AI do for my business" / "AI tools small business"; LinkedIn outreach with a generic AI-savings hook; cold outreach to ICPs with no prior pain signal | Audience B dominant | **Path A-bridge (v1) → graduating to Path A.** Do NOT substitute Path B as a fallback for Path A — Path B does not answer Audience B's question. If Path A-bridge isn't feasible today, defer the second video until it is. |
| Mixed | Combination of the above | Both | **Default to Path A-bridge when unclear.** The cost of a discovery visitor bouncing on inscrutable product-cam is high; the inverse cost is low, because recognition buyers reading aspirational future-state content self-skip to the CTA without bouncing. Discovery copy is forgiving in a way recognition copy isn't. |

## Free Thinker's Vision

From FT-R3 (round 10): *"A Tuesday in 2026 vs. a Tuesday in 2027. Same owner, same Tuesday, same coffee, same office. Left frame: 2026 — the owner you are today, doing the 47 small jobs nobody pays you to do. Right frame: 2027 — same owner, same desk, but the 47 jobs are visibly happening without him. He's on the phone with his daughter. He's reading. He's leaving early. No voiceover, no music swell, no 'imagine if.' Just two columns of the same life, one year apart, with the agent's work happening as ambient texture in the 2027 frame — a notification slides in, an invoice gets paid, a review gets answered, all in the background while the owner does something else."*

FT's three reasons it works for discovery (R3): *"(1) Teaches without explaining. The owner who's never heard of agents sees what they do by watching them do it. No 'AI' jargon, no 'agents' terminology — observable cause-and-effect. (2) Triggers aspiration, not recognition. Pain audience asks 'is this me?' Discovery audience asks 'could that be me?' Same lever, different timeline. (3) Still product-as-evidence. The 'ambient agent work' in the 2027 frame is real screen content composited into the live-action scene — we extend Grounder's axis across time rather than abandon it."*

From FT-R4 distinguishing from IDEA_01: *"Time-travel answers: 'What does my life look like if I have this?' Future-tense. Real product footage plus the operator's life around it (closed laptop, kid pickup, dinner). Conversion lever = aspiration. Converts buyers who haven't yet decided they want the thing. Different cognitive jobs. Different funnel positions. You need both."*

From FT-R4's longitudinal-capture defense: *"What keeps time-travel as product-as-evidence (not fiction): the agent work in the 2027 frame is real screen content (real Slack, invoices, replies) composited into live-action. The only 'fictional' element is 'this is the same person a year apart' — a framing device, not a fabrication. It's a longitudinal capture, not a staged before/after."*

The clearest one-line distinction FT made (R3 re-send): *"In time-travel, every pixel showing the agent doing work is real product. In a scenario ad, every pixel is invented."* And the cleanest framing of why the discovery cut hooks an unfamiliar audience: *"Curiosity, not pain, is the entry point."*

On the FT-5 kill (R3 re-send, sharper than R4's compressed version): *"Threat-based ads don't work in B2B SaaS for unrecognized categories. The pain has to be felt before the threat lands; the threat doesn't create the felt pain."*

On the FT-3 absorption format reasoning (R3 re-send): *"Documentary frame requires the viewer to commit 30 seconds before the reveal pays off. Landing pages don't get 30 seconds — they get 6. The reveal arrives after the bounce."* The "keep frame, drop format" move preserves the named-notification convention with FT's three example signatures: *"Maya replied to a Yelp review. / Carlos drafted the invoice. / Sam rebooked Thursday's no-show."*

## Grounder's Honest Read

### Why this one

From Thread E (round 11) — Grounder arrived at the same answer from a different direction: *"Revelation works through specificity, not montage. 'You know that Tuesday-night QuickBooks ritual? Here's an agent doing it while you're at your kid's soccer game.' One ultra-specific scenario, named precisely, then absence-of-AI counterfactual."* FT-R4 then killed the counterfactual half and left the specificity half as the operating principle; Grounder's revelation-via-specificity and FT's TT are the same construct.

From Grounder's audience-scoped walkback of evidence-only: *"Walking back part of 'performance vs evidence': evidence-only is right for A, wrong for B. Two-video setup isn't stylistic indulgence — it's two different cognitive tasks."* This walkback is what created the structural space for this brief to exist as separate from IDEA_01.

### How it connects to the brief

Honours the seed's "Amazing world of AI" instinct without falling into trope-fatigue. Audience B is the only justified second video per Grounder's two-personas test (different persona, not just different emotional framing). The page's recognition vs discovery routing — invisible at the URL level via IDEA_03's UX-C — fulfils the operator's "Is this you?" intuition without the friction of a quiz that adds a click before value.

### Where it could lose people

- **Longitudinal-capture honesty.** Path A requires real consented customers and a real 12-month timeline. If the operator stages it as "same day, two outfits" and presents it as longitudinal, that's the produced-ad-cosplaying-leaked-artifact failure mode that Grounder named for IDEA_01. Path A-bridge exists specifically so the operator can ship the construct without violating the honesty claim.
- **Path A-bridge drift.** Path A-bridge's "future-state visualisation" framing must not be conflated with Path A's longitudinal claim. The copy on a bridge production must read "what your Tuesday could look like, this time next year" — never "twelve months later" or "this happened in 12 months." That documentary phrasing is earned only by Path A's longitudinal capture.
- **Path B substitution risk (the misfire to prevent).** Path B is the right answer when Audience B is a minority of traffic. It is NOT a discovery-video; it is a recognition-deepening. If the operator has discovery-dominant channel mix and Path A-bridge isn't feasible today, the temptation will be to ship Path B as "the ship-now option." Don't. **Defer the second video entirely until Path A-bridge is feasible.** Shipping Path B to a discovery-dominant audience produces a recognition-deepening for an audience that needs revelation — wrong job, not a smaller version of the right one.
- **Path A as graduation target, not first-class option.** Path A's prerequisites (paying customer running today + consent for two shoots 12 months apart + 12-month wait) make it a fantasy with a timeline for a solo operator at the landing-page stage. Path A is what Path A-bridge becomes after 12 months of customer deployment — same 2026 capture, real 2027 re-shoot swapped in, longitudinal documentary copy earned. The graduation moment is a credibility upgrade, not a re-production.
- **Production overload.** This brief shares footage with IDEA_01 but adds substantial post-production (annotations, named-notification labels, time-jump composition, voiceover beats, ambient-texture compositing). For a solo operator, this is more work than IDEA_01 in absolute terms. Don't underestimate; budget at least 2x IDEA_01's post-production time. Grounder's editorial read (R3 reshape Q1) made the structural breakdown explicit: *"(a) Shot inventory genuinely shared. Spreadsheet-cam capturing 6 hours of agent work yields enough raw material for both a 30s recognition cut and a 90s discovery cut. Real production win. (b) Cuts need different on-screen elements. Recognition cut leans on screen as-is (fluent buyer). Discovery cut needs annotations, labels, named-notification convention (FT-3 essence), time-jump frame (TT) — i.e., recognition footage + visual decoder layer. Decoder = Lottie overlays, kinetic labels, diptych composition. Non-trivial post. (c) Audio fully separate. Recognition cut: no/minimal VO. Discovery cut: operator voiceover walking the viewer. Different scripts, recording sessions, tonal calibrations."* The "film once, ship twice" framing under-budgets the discovery cut by exactly the audio + decoder layers.

## Open Questions

1. **Which path?** Tier 1 analytics if available, Tier 2 channel-mix fallback otherwise. Operator's ICP and current acquisition mix determine the answer.
2. **Longitudinal viability for Path A.** Does the operator have a paying customer they could film today AND a credible commitment from that customer to be filmed again 12 months later? If yes, Path A is the strongest version. If no, Path A-bridge is the v1 ship and the operator can graduate to Path A later if they capture the 2026 footage now.
3. **Length.** Pain cut: 6 seconds (FT's stated number; non-negotiable). Discovery cut: 15–30 seconds (curiosity hook needs more bandwidth). Dual frame: 30–60 seconds. The Explorer-research 30s sweet spot applies to the discovery cut and the dual frame as the visitor's first conscious watch.
4. **Path A-bridge honesty copy.** What exactly does the headline say to communicate "future state" without claiming "12 months later"? Suggested: "What your Tuesday could look like by next year." Not: "Twelve months later." Specific copy needs operator review.
5. **Named-notification convention (from FT-3 absorbed).** Does the agent actually have a name in the deployed product, or is the name a marketing artefact? If the latter, copy must not imply the agent is named *Maya* if the product calls it *the assistant*. Either name the agent in the product or use a generic in the video.
6. **Voiceover or none.** FT-R4's TT has no voiceover for the dual frame itself. Path B is voiceover-led. If the operator's analytics push toward Path B but the operator's voice/comfort on camera is weak, the brief weakens. Mitigation rules: a hired narrator for Path B is acceptable. An actor playing the operator for Path A is NOT acceptable (breaks the longitudinal claim). For Path A-bridge, operator-as-narrator is preferred but optional (no voiceover, or operator's own voice for an introductory beat over the 2026 frame); an actor playing the operator violates the future-state-visualisation construct the same way it would violate Path A's documentary construct.

7. **Named-notification names — brand and demographic check.** FT's example signatures *"Maya replied to a Yelp review. / Carlos drafted the invoice. / Sam rebooked Thursday's no-show"* read as warm and diverse for a mainstream SMB audience. Two operator-side considerations: (a) the names should match (or at least not conflict with) any real product-naming the operator has decided on for the agent — if the product calls the agent "the assistant" or has a different launch name, the video's named signatures may need to use that or a generic ("an agent rebooked Thursday's no-show"); (b) if the operator's ICP skews to a specific regional or demographic market (e.g. rural Midwest contractors, suburban East-Asian-American SMBs), the names should land plausibly there. Names are a 30-second copy decision, not a brand-line decision — easy to tune, but worth tuning deliberately rather than defaulting to FT's examples.

8. **Interval calibration — is 12 months the right time-jump for this product?** FT-R4 stated 12 months as the interval between the two frames. It works as a default because it matches the buyer's mental model of annual review and gives the agent's compounding effect time to be visible. But the right interval depends on the operator's actual product timeline. Products with multi-month onboarding or slow adoption curves may need 18 months to be visibly transformed. Products with immediate productive use (the agent does work day one) may show enough change at 3 or 6 months that 12 months feels like overshooting the credible delta. Operator should sanity-check 12 months against their actual deployment-to-stable-value timeline before locking the headline copy and (for Path A) the customer-shoot scheduling.
7. **Routing to dual frame.** Click-through (visible CTA on the single-frame cuts) vs scroll-reveal vs auto-advance after the 6/15-second loop completes one cycle. Each has different conversion implications; none has direct A/B evidence from Explorer's research. Operator A/B tests after ship.
