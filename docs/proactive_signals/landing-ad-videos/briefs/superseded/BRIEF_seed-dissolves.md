## Summary

_[Pre-load.]_

This brief argues a structural position rather than a video aesthetic: the seed's central UX premise — two parallel videos surfaced via an "Is this you?" routing mechanic (quiz / card chooser / branching narrative) — is the wrong shape for the deliverable. A buyer who recognises their own spreadsheet hell in the first three seconds of a product-evidence hero does not need to be routed; the video itself is the recognition. The seed's "Is this you?" language is preserved as a one-line headline above the hero, but the routing mechanic dissolves. A second video is justified only when it serves a genuinely different persona (e.g. service-business owner vs e-commerce owner, with materially different products visible on screen), not when it serves a different emotional framing of the same persona.

_[Add: what replaces the routing UX — single product-cam hero + sharper headline + below-fold scroll to longer explainer + optionally a demoted UX-A "pick your pain" router pointing to case studies, not videos. The decision rule for when two videos are justified.]_

## What Makes This Interesting

_[Pre-load.]_

Most ideation sessions on a seed produce idea briefs that elaborate the seed. This brief contradicts the seed at its load-bearing point. Two independent lines of evidence converge here: Explorer's research showing quiz-routed heroes have zero direct A/B evidence and add a click before the value prop is visible, and Grounder's structural argument that an undeniable product-evidence video collapses the problem the routing UX was meant to solve. Naming this contradiction explicitly is more valuable to the operator than smoothing it over with a hybrid recommendation, because the contradiction is the actionable insight: don't build the quiz, don't write the two scripts, sharpen one video and one headline.
