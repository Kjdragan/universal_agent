## Summary

_[Pre-load. To fill on convergence.]_

The landing-page hero is a 30-second muted-autoplay-loop screen recording of the real product doing real work on real data — not a polished produced ad, not a cinematic, not an animated illustration. The lo-fi quality is the proof: the buyer sees the agent's cursor move on the operator's actual spreadsheet/inbox/dashboard and recognises both their own working environment and the relief of seeing it work itself. Style decisions (cartoon vs photoreal vs anything else) dissolve because the question stops being aesthetic and becomes evidentiary.

## What Makes This Interesting

_[Pre-load.]_

The dominant 2026 hero-pattern data (Digital Applied 2,000-page study: video-autoplay -7%, Lottie/SVG +5%, single-stat +18%) suggests video is the wrong deliverable. The product-as-evidence hero is the narrow case where video *beats* the alternatives — because Lottie/SVG can't prove the agent works and a single-stat hero can't *show* the recognition moment. The lift comes from collapsing two normally-separate things (proof and recognition) into one frame.
