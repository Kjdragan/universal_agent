## Distilled Brief

**Project:** Advertisement videos for the landing page of a business-owner-targeted AI/agents service.

**Initial scenario hypotheses (two):**

1. **"Amazing world of AI"** — introduce a business owner who doesn't know what AI agents and automation can do today. Excitement / discovery hook.
2. **"Struggling business owner"** — relatable pain scenario, then relief via agents. Empathy / recognition hook.

**Landing-page integration concept:** An "Is this you?" section near the top of the landing page that surfaces both videos. Mechanic unspecified.

## Open Questions to Interrogate

- **Style:** photorealistic vs cartoon. Operator leans cartoon but is worried it will read as childish / dated rather than modern. Worth interrogating: is there a third style? What does "modern cartoon" look like in 2026 for B2B SaaS?
- **Scenario count:** Two is the operator's first instinct. Should there be more? Fewer? Different framings entirely?
- **"Is this you?" UX:** Parallel autoplay? Quiz that routes to one video? Card chooser? Branching narrative?
- **Production pipeline:** Veo / Sora / Runway / Remotion+AI assets / hand-animated / mixed media — what makes iteration cheap?
- **Length(s):** 15s social cut? 30s landing-page hero? 60s explainer? Probably multiple cuts.
- **Tone:** playful vs authoritative vs understated-confident.

## Explicit Asks for Free Thinker

Push for unexpected stylistic directions beyond cartoon vs photoreal — stylized 3D, mixed media, deliberately lo-fi, kinetic typography hybrids, documentary-feel, vector/SVG, paper-cutout, motion-design abstract, etc. Push for unexpected scenario framings — "future self" device, day-in-the-life contrast, customer-facing angle rather than owner-facing, the agent itself as narrator, the absence-of-AI counterfactual.

## Explicit Asks for Grounder

Push back on:

- Production cost and iteration speed per style.
- Brand risk of cartoon-reading-childish for a B2B audience.
- "AI ad about AI" trope fatigue — every AI startup is making the same hero video right now.
- Conversion-rate evidence for video-on-landing-pages (and where it falls down).
- Whether two separate videos actually outperforms one well-targeted one with strong copy.
- Whether the "Is this you?" framing makes the operator look like a generic ad rather than a precise solution.

## Session Goal

Produce 2–3 concrete idea briefs covering: scenario list, style direction, length/format set, production pipeline recommendation, and landing-page UX integration. NOT to build yet. This is a grooming session to align before construction.
