## The Original Concept

The operator brought a hypothesis to the table: build advertisement video(s) for the landing page of a B2B-SaaS / AI-agents service targeting small/mid business owners. Their first instinct was two videos — an "Amazing world of AI" discovery hook for owners who don't know what agents can do, and a "Struggling business owner" empathy hook for owners who already feel the pain — surfaced near the top of the landing page via an "Is this you?" mechanic.

The operator leaned cartoon for the aesthetic but worried it would read as childish / dated for a B2B audience. They flagged three additional anxieties up front: "AI ad about AI" trope fatigue, the possibility that two diffuse videos would underperform one well-targeted video with sharp copy, and the risk that the "Is this you?" framing would feel generic rather than precise. They explicitly asked for a grooming session before any production work — interrogate assumptions, then build.

The session was scoped to produce 2–3 idea briefs, then a vision document. NOT to build.

## Emerging Themes

- **The three-axis stack.** Cartoon-vs-photoreal (operator) → produced-vs-leaked (FT) → performance-vs-evidence (Grounder, later audience-scoped to evidence-for-A / revelation-for-B). Each layer dissolved the prior layer's apparent disagreements by reframing one level up. The vision document opens with this stack as the conceptual frame — it's why the operator's original style anxiety was the right question asked at the wrong altitude.
- **One capture, two productions, shared raw footage.** Replaced the earlier (incorrect) framing of "two cuts of one asset." The footage layer is shared at the production-pipeline level; the recognition cut and the discovery cut are distinct post-productions with materially different deliverables. This terminology is locked across all three briefs and the vision doc.
- **Composition devices vs aesthetic styles vs UX mechanics are different axes.** Grounder's clean-up move from Thread D — the dialogue had been comparing apples (FT-6 kinetic typography) to oranges (FT-2 diptych composition) to platters (UX-A quiz mechanic) as if they were alternatives. Separating these axes means a brief can stack one of each into a coherent idea without forcing false either/ors.
- **Crossing convergence as the strongest signal.** FT-R3 (round 10) and Grounder-R3 (round 11) were dual-sent within five minutes of each other, neither having read the other's R3. They independently arrived at the same three-bet structure and the same operator-capability fallback ladder from opposite starting points (FT from style; Grounder from cognitive task). When two dialogue agents reach the same answer from opposite directions, the answer is durable.
- **Operator-capability is upstream of every video brief.** Grounder's R3 pushback — *"That's a production stack, not a product stack"* — elevated this from a brief footnote to a vision-document-level concern. The brief inventory is gated by operator's product maturity, not their production stack.
- **The seed's instinct survives in the language and dies in the mechanic.** "Is this you?" survives as a one-line headline (the part that carried information); the quiz/routing mechanic dies (the part that carried inherited landing-page convention). The vision document is explicit about which half of the seed survives.

## What This Session Did NOT Explore

- **Paid distribution and social cuts.** The session is scoped to the landing-page deliverable. 15-second social variants, paid-Meta cuts, YouTube pre-roll variants, LinkedIn newsfeed-format cuts were not designed. Re-cuts from the same raw footage layer are economically straightforward but were not specified.
- **A/B test design for the v1 hero.** The vision document calls for measuring against a Lottie/SVG control over 4–6 weeks before promoting to UX-C v1.5; the actual test design (sample size, conversion lift threshold, secondary metrics like watch-with-sound rate) was not specified.
- **Demoted UX-A case-study content.** The router needs 3–5 written case studies pointed at it before launch. The case-study authoring brief is downstream of this session — IDEA_03 names it as a v1 prerequisite, not a v2 nicety, but the content itself was not produced.
- **Product-naming for the agent.** FT-R4's "agent has a name" convention (Maya replies to a Yelp review, Carlos drafts the invoice) implies a naming decision for the product itself. The session did not produce names; it preserved the convention as an option for the operator's product team.
- **Engineering ownership for sub-1.5s LCP.** Every video brief requires this; the engineering owner is named as an Open Question in IDEA_01 but not resolved.
- **Q3 2027 Path A longitudinal-capture customer pipeline.** Path A's defense relies on filming a real customer today and re-filming them 12 months later. Identifying which customer, scheduling both shoots, and securing consent for both is downstream work not done in this session.
- **YouTube tutorial integration.** The repo has a "YouTube demo unification" deferred initiative; this session did not engage with whether the landing-page videos relate to that pipeline.
